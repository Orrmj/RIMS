Title Modelling and optimization of lead-acid batteries on rolling stock applications: A metaheuristic approach through big data analysis.
 
Author De Oliveira, AF
Dutra, JC
Affiliation MRS Logística S.A., Juiz de Fora, Brazil
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th International Heavy Haul Conference, (IHHA 2019), Achieving breakthrough performance levels, 12-14 June, 2019, Narvik, Norway
Publication Date 2019
Abstract This work carries out simulations from the extraction of the parameters of lead-acid batteries on rolling stock applications, with the main objective of determining the maximum time limit that the system can be stressed out during the discharge process without compromising the locomotive’s operations. This study presents, through Big Data analysis, predictions in different discharging scenarios, using a trimester of telemetry data of a locomotive operation in the parameterization process. The method used in the modelling allows the extraction of the internal impedance parameters from a statistical and equivalent circuit model. In addition, it also allows the elaboration of Monte Carlo Simulation on the probability analysis of the data collected according to the initial state of charge of the analysed battery. The results are obtained by means of optimization using genetic algorithms, through the initial information of the state of charge, battery capacity, temperature, input voltage and terminal current of the system. The performance comparison and the accuracy between the simulated data and the data collected from the real operation allow us to verify the model.

Title Research on optimization of heavy haul train speed protection algorithm based on improved braking model
 
Author Liu, Y
Yuan, L
Wei, G
Affiliation Beijing Jiaotong University, Beijing, China
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th International Heavy Haul Conference, (IHHA 2019), Achieving breakthrough performance levels, 12-14 June, 2019, Narvik, Norway
Publication Date 2019
Abstract With the application and research of moving block technology in heavy haul train control, higher requirements are put forward for the accuracy of heavy haul train speed protection curve. During the operation of heavy haul trains, the load and the braking rate changes greatly under various working conditions, which makes the actual braking model parameters fluctuate, resulting in a large deviation in the calculation of the speed protection algorithm, the train interval increases and operating efficiency decreases. How to overcome random factors interference, obtain train parameters in real time, and establish accurate train dynamics model online are important to improve the accuracy of train speed protection. Based on the analysis of the braking process of heavy haul trains and the influence of train-related parameters on braking distance, an improved multi-point train dynamics model is proposed in this paper. By collecting historical data of existing lines, the model of heavy haul train braking is constructed by the experimental modelling method of least squares support vector machine (LS-LVM) to solve the changes of train braking distance with the initial braking speed. Combined with the line conditions, the train speed protection curve with improved dynamic model is constructed, and then simulated under two train formation schemes with the selected actual line data, the experimental result verifies that this algorithm can achieve more precise train operation control under the premise of ensuring driving safety, shortening the running interval and improving the operating efficiency of heavy haul trains.

Title A planning system for locomotive scheduling
 
Author Drott, J
Hesselberg, E
Kohl, N
Kremer, M
Affiliation Swedish State Railways, Stockholm, Sweden
Carmen Systems AB, Gothenburg, Sweden
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract Scheduling locomotives in a large railway network is a complex and time-consuming task. The quality and efficiency of planning and scheduling are becoming increasingly important since railway companies will have to answer to demands on higher productivity and stronger competition. Together with the Swedish State Railways (SJ), Carmen Systems AB have developed Carmen Fleet - an advanced locomotive scheduling system. The automatic scheduling module of Carmen Fleet is based on a network model and generates optimal or close to optimal solutions. The system minimizes the number of locomotives, but other costs and the quality of the solution can also be considered. Carmen Fleet can also solve problems with multiple types of locomotives. In this case a decomposition technique known as Lagrangean relaxation is applied to determine the assignment of locomotive types to tasks. All constraints and costs are described with a specialized computer language, the Carmen Rule Language, to make the dialog between the user and system easy. The user can change all costs and constraints of the problem and has full control of how the network is defined. The system also includes an interactive module where the user can make manual changes to the schedule, print reports and analyze using different graphical views. The user can control all input and easily try out different scenarios by making simulated changes to the timetable, costs and other parameters. SJ plans to take Carmen Fleet into production by the end of 1997, but a prototype has been tested on production data using a production ruleset written by the planners at SJ. These tests indicates savings of more than 4% on the number of locomotives needed to operate the timetable. Also the solutions produced by Carmen Fleet are superior to the manual reference solution with respect to a number of other criteria. In addition to the direct savings, Carmen Fleet will decrease the time needed to make a locomotive plan for a particular timetable. This makes it possible to simulate the effects of timetable changes, facilitates the further integration of the different planning tasks and make it possible to respond to market demands more quickly.

Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract In traction applications a fast torque control is required; it can be obtained by using vector control strategies; i.e. field orientation schemes (FO) and direct self-control (DSC). The implementation of the proposed control schemes is no longer a problem because low cost microcontrollers are available along with voltage source inverters. The aim of this paper is to analyse the behaviour of the controlled drives in the case of a conventional induction motor. The amplitude of the pulsating component of torque as well as the machine current distorsion are monitored and compared in the case of the two different control strategies. Simulations were carried out considering the induction motor used in the ETR460 (a high speed locomotive of Italian railways) with a rated power of 550 kW. All experimental results are verified with the help of a developed user-friendly package for the analysis of controlled electrical machines linkable with Simulink, the dynamic simulator by the Mathworks.

Title Angle of arrival (AOA) determination system for railway on-board positioning
 
Author Arenas, M
Podhorski, A
Goya, J
Sedano, B
Arrizabalaga, S
Mendizabal, J
Affiliation Ceit and Tecnun (University of Navarra), Donostia, Spain
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract In the last few years, the railway sector has realized the importance of a more efficient use of the existing railways infrastructures. The heavily congested tracks are being used below their maximum capability. The latest advances to solve this problem focus on migrating from ETCS level 2 to level 3. ETCS level 3 is defined in [1] as "similar to level 2 but with train location and train integrity supervision based on information received from the train". Hence, the advances include the use of new onboard location system that should overcome the requirements of the level 3 migration. Nonetheless, the migration to ETCS level 3, which maximizes the railway efficiency, has not been yet foreseen due to technical constraints as stated in [2]. The main drawback detected in the onboard location systems in the railway industry is the inability of providing a real time train position in every point of the tracks. GNSS systems, the most widespread technologies to position globally suffer from coverage problems mainly in urban environments, as well as in some environment orography such as canyons or tunnels. In this context, the contribution of EATS project (FP7 TRANSPORT 314219) is to develop a smart train positioning system (STPS) [3]. This system takes advantage of the combination of different techniques, such as GNSS and the AoA of the GSMR antennas, to determine the real time train position. This paper shows the important advance in AoA estimation system with signals received from the GSMR antennas. For that, the two main developments have consisted of: 1. Develop and implement an AoA estimation system based on MUSIC algorithm under FPGA technology. 2. Develop and implement an AoA estimation system simulation tool in MATLAB. The AoA estimation system makes the most of the full GSMR coverage required in ETCS level 2. Therefore, this technology will show a full coverage and will be controlled by the railway itself. The AoA estimation system will be composed of the implementation of the MUSIC algorithm under FPGAs technology, an antenna array and their respective frontends. The MUSIC algorithm allows the estimation of the AoA of multiple signals incident on an antenna array. The MATLAB simulation tool shapes the whole AoA estimation system. For that, several different blocks, which model different parts of the system, have been created. In that way, it is possible to configure the different components that take part in the AoA calculation. Hence, it is possible to estimate the best configuration for the whole system. Furthermore, this simulation platform is used as a validation tool for the real implementation of the AoA estimation system. Also, it has to be designed and implemented an automatic validation system of the whole AoA design. This work is part of the FP7 project EATS that proposes a step forward in increasing the availability of the realtime train positioning in every point of the track.

Author Latour, M
Nuendel, M
Affiliation Siemens AG
Source Railway Technical Society of Australasia (RTSA)
Conference CORE 2008, Rail; the core of integrated transport, Conference on Railway Engineering, Perth, Western Australia, 2008
Publication Date 2008
Abstract March 2003 Australia’s largest provider of freight transport, Queensland Rail, entered into a contract with Siemens for a traction system upgrade of three 3100/3200 Class electric locomotives into prototype 3700 Class locomotives. After successful trial runs, where the locomotives demonstrated that 3 units equipped with Siemens new AC traction systems can pull the same train previously hauled by 5 locomotives with DC traction system, the railway took up the option of the initial contract to equip further 60 units in mid 2005. In order to keep up with the increasing demand for traction power, 20 new 3800 Class electric locomotives were ordered in March 2006 and this order was extended to 45 units in mid 2007. This article explains the motives, main requirements and performance criteria for the upgrade and the purchase of new locomotives with Siemens AC traction systems. Focus is given to the description of the new traction system components as well as the features that enable significant advantages and benefits over the conventional units. Overall, the performance criteria for the new AC traction systems were met or exceeded. Both, the refurbished and the new locomotives will not only provide major economical advantages to the customer, but also improve reliability, cab ergo reliability, cab ergonomics and facilities for the drivers, in addition to significantly reducing the impact on the environment.

Title A train traction optimizer and power reduction device
 
Author de Morais, ER
Affiliation CVRD Companhia Vale do Rio Doce
Source CRC for Rail Innovation
Conference CORE 2006, Rail achieving growth, Conference on Railway Engineering, Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, 30 April-3 May 2006
Publication Date 2006
Abstract One of the goals that all Brazilian railroads look for is how to improve the train energy efficiency, because this item impacts directly on the expenses of the company and in the final price of the product for customers. Then all and any project that seeks to improve this item usually receives a good acceptance for the Board of the Company. The price of fuel (diesel oil) represents a big one percentile of the overall railroad transportation cost, when operating with diesel-electric locomotives. CVRD (Companhia Vale do Rio Doce) worries a lot about fuel economy and it motivates the accomplishment and development of some experiments for economy of fuel. This project called OTRP - The Train Traction Optimiser and Power Reduction Device has two main objectives: the first one is to give the train engineer some tools (through commands of train operation) to improve the performance of his operation/and the second one is to make possible that the locomotive can work in the mode 'Pace Control' or 'Slow Speed' or still 'power reduction'. This practice of power reduction is used in the processes of loading trains at low speeds. The function pace control or slow speed is already an integral part of the modern locomotives. But the purpose here is to equip the older locomotives with this very important function.

Title QR national AC diesel-electric locomotives for the Hunter Valley
 
Author Haywood, G
Bartlett, S
Affiliation United Group Rail
Source CRC for Rail Innovation
Conference CORE 2006, Rail achieving growth, Conference on Railway Engineering, Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, 30 April-3 May 2006
Publication Date 2006
Abstract United Group Rail (UGL Rail) has designed and manufactured, in partnership with GE - Transportation, their first Australian built AC Diesel Electric Locomotives for QRNational (5000 class or C40aci type). These locomotives were designed for heavy haul coal operations in the Hunter Valley of NSW. The haulage task is two 5000 class locomotives hauling 74 coal hoppers at 120 tonnes each from Mt Arthur mine to Kooragang or Port Waratah coal unloaders. Testing has confirmed the locomotive design capability. The new C40aci locomotive design is another significant example of the UGL Rail/GE - Transportation partnership delivering a successful solution to their client. This solution has enabled improved train efficiency with more responsive train brakes, an increased payload (18% increase in trailing load for AC) and reduced maintenance costs for QRNational (less locomotives to perform the haulage task). By maximising the service mass for the AC locomotives from 150 tonnes to 180 tonnes enabled an increase of 20% in trailing load capability but also for this particular application reduced the adhesion demand for a better train reliability. This paper will detail the major challenges associated with the locomotive design, primarily with the minimum of changes to adapt the existing Cv40-9i ('NR' class) DC locomotive design into an AC locomotive configuration.

Title An optimization approach to the locomotive scheduling problem in Brazilian railways
 
Author Baceler, A
Garcia, A
Affiliation CVRD Companhia Vale do Rio Doce
Source CRC for Rail Innovation
Conference CORE 2006, Rail achieving growth, Conference on Railway Engineering, Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, 30 April-3 May 2006
Publication Date 2006
Abstract Locomotives are among the most significant railway operations expenses and capital cost. In many Brazilian Railways, the locomotive assignment to trains are totally defined by the expertise of employees who works for those companies without any computational tools based on operational research concepts. This results in poor locomotive scheduling and the consequences are observed in the utilisation and productivity metrics. Research has been conducted to identify the related works developed in the locomotive scheduling and locomotive assignment context and the use of operational research techniques to develop an optimisation approach that guarantees the needs related to the real problem of Brazilian Railways. This paper presents the developments that have resulted in a mathematical model to solve the locomotive scheduling problem in Brazilian Railways. A case study is discussed, where the resulting model has been applied in the Vitoria-Minas Railroad (EFVM), owned by Companhia Vale do Rio Doce (CVRD) and is one of the most important and productive in Brazil. Finally, the results make possible to view the main differences between a real-life locomotive assignment and the locomotive scheduling based on the proposed optimisation approach.

Title Improving the performance of a locomotive components maintenance facility
 
Author Donaggio, A
Trinkel, R
Rabbi, C
Affiliation CVRD Companhia Vale do Rio Doce
Source CRC for Rail Innovation
Conference CORE 2006, Rail achieving growth, Conference on Railway Engineering, Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, 30 April-3 May 2006
Publication Date 2006
Abstract This project is aligned with one of the main guidelines for growth of CVRD, which priorities excellence in managing its assets. The entire components maintenance shop structure was based on a need for service and availability of spare parts to support increased demand of the locomotive maintenance shops, a demand generated by the change in focus of these sites and increased production necessary along the transport corridors. To meet this need, new values were necessary, thus determining a new culture. This new culture transformed a Maintenance Shop into a Components Reconditioning Unit, changing the focus from 'shop' to 'factory'. To support implementation of this new culture, a working base was defined with four pillars: people/quality/time/cost. Priorities were set for each of these pillars, with the consequent use of the PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) management tool. Currently, each of the pillars is in the implementation phase of the PDCA. These will be detailed in this work separated in two levels. The first PDCA includes all the cycle and have been performed in the first year, while the second PDCA has just begun.

Title Locomotive noise synopsis
 
Author Matthews, C
Affiliation United Goninan
Source Railway Technical Society of Australasia (RTSA)
Conference CORE 2004, New Horizons for Rail, Conference on Railway Engineering, Darwin, Northern Territory, Australia, June 20-23, 2004
Publication Date 2004
Abstract Since the 1980s the issue of noise generated by the rail industry has gained increasing attention. The various train operators, in response to environmental pressures, have gradually introduced noise level restrictions to the rolling stock that they have purchased and have even retrofitted some rolling stock with noise abatement equipment. Locomotives have been identified for particular attention due to the large noise differential that exists between the locomotive and freight wagons/passenger cars. By nominating separate noise controls for locomotives, more realistic noise limits can be established and, at the same time, still maintain control of wagon and passenger car noise levels. The purpose of this paper is to outline some of the issues surrounding the noise control of locomotives. The first part of the paper contains a review of the terms used in the study of noise and the background to setting noise level limitations and the second part of the paper describes the locomotive noise modelling process generally adopted by the industry.

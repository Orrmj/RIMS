Title A telediagnostic platform for rail fleet management: challenges and opportunities for development
 
Author Favo, F
Munizza, S
Vergata, M
Affiliation Rete Ferroviaria Italiana Spa, Napoli, Italia
Rete Ferroviaria Italiana Spa, Catanzaro, Italia
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract The introduction of telediagnostic systems on rail vehicles makes it possible for fleet managers to optimise operation, maintenance and engineering of railway fleets. The reason for this research is to make an initial valuation of the possible benefits that may derive from the installation of telediagnostic systems on the entire fleet of vehicles managed by RFI (currently consisting of about 9500 units), starting from a first experiment recently conducted at the RFI’s National Maintenance Workshop in Catanzaro. The major difficulties for the extension of the model were related to mission profiles and machine age heterogeneity. These difficulties, however, are reducible after a careful rationalization of the park. In addition to “standard” telediagnostic systems features, which anyway would bring a very significant innovation to RFI’s rolling stock management processes in terms of quality and cost reduction, the conceived prototype, by taking successive steps, will be also able to inform RFI’s fleet managers about real use of equipments, in order to rationalize the fleet; to program condition-based maintenance operations; to forbid improper or unsafe use, improving Safety and Security levels; to monitor environmental indicators (particulate emissions, fuel and lubricants leakage, etc); to be compliant with reports provided by Regulation EU/445/2011 (Annex III Section 7.4); to lay the groundwork for telemaintenance; to make data more systematically available for international collaboration between Railway companies and manufacturers; to transform some machines in drones by a specific application used to replicate driver desk commands in Control Room. This last futuristic function will enable RFI to effect rail inspection/reopening without danger to maintenance operators in the case of natural hazards, attacks, etc.

Title Coating system 2000
 
Author Kruger, J
Affiliation Deutsche Bohn AG, Brandenburg, Germany
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract Railway vehicles are given a colourful appearance and protected against corrosion, as well as operating and weather effects, by coating them with organic paints. In spite of the considerable time and cost involved in applying and drying the paintwork, the behaviour of the coatings in railway service, with the exception of the corrosion protection, is often less than satisfactory. In a research project called Coating system 2000 procedures and materials are being developed, which should improve both the resistance properties and the optical quality of the surface, make it easier to clean and considerably reduce the application times and costs. The objectives of the project are to develop a tough, elastic transparent coating with an inorganic-organic basis (nanocomposite coating) with extremely high mechanical and chemical resistance and easy-to-clean properties, and the use of new types of self adhesive foils as a complete or partial substitute for the paintwork. The nanocomposite coating is obtained by spraying a sol onto a primed surface and subsequently thermal hardening the gel produced. The sol is produced by hydrolysis and partial condensation of glycidylalkoxysilanes with the addition of nanoscale metal oxides and other substances. By incorporation of perfluoralkylalkoxysilanes in the inorganic-organic network a low energy surface with easy to clean properties can be produced by enrichment of fluorsilane at the interface gel/air. First practical tests showed the high resistance of the nanocomposite coating but the properties of a antiadhesive surface were not so pronounced. The further material development is done with respect to the optimisation of the mechanical and chemical resistance, so that simultaneous an optimum cleaning ability is produced by the formation of an extremely resistant and smooth surface. The main reason for the use of self-adhesive foils is the reduction of the application time because it is not necessary to apply the top coat which provides the colour. The foils presently available need to be optimised for flexibility in attachment, removability during repairs and redesign and increasing the durability properties. Besides sticking them all over the surface of coach external body parts it is also possible to achieve design features, such us, for instance, coloured side stripes on an single colour paint scheme. The combination of the two developments is conceivable, as the nanocomposite material could be applied on a coloured water-borne primer and after hardening, for instance, the side stripes could be produced by foils.

Title An overall innovative numerical method for the simulation of the fatigue crack initiation and propagation in rails for their maintenance optimization
 
Author Nguyen-Tajan, TM-L
Funfschilling, C
Mai, SH
Trolle, B
Affiliation SNCF, Innovation & research Department, Paris, France
SNCF Réseau, Maintenance Department, Paris, France
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract This paper deals with the rolling fatigue cracking appearing on the surface or sub-surface of rails, called squats and head-checks. These cracks are induced by the repeated passes of the train wheels. They can propagate and lead to the rail fracture if they are not detected soon enough and if the rail is not replaced on time. To ensure security and avoid train derailments, heavy monitoring and complex maintenance processes are applied, including grindings, repairs and replacements. Such maintenance is very expensive so it is a big challenge to optimize it and make it more cost-effective. To have a better understanding of the physical mechanisms, an overall innovative modelling method has been developed. It includes the dynamical simulation of a train circulating on a track, the calculation of the wheel/rail contact forces, the determination of the 3D cyclic plastic stresses in the rail, its fatigue analysis and the modelling of the crack propagation. A short description of the multi-scale modelling strategy is given in the first section of the paper. In the second section, a first application of the modelling tools is described.

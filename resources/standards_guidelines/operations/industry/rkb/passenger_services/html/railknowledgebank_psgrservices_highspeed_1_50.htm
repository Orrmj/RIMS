Title Development of new brake system and control method for future railways
 
Author Kawaguchi, K
Watanabe, T
Kakinuma, H
Tamaoki, T
Affiliation Railway Technical Research Institute, Tokyo, Japan
Hokkaido Railway Company, Sapporo, Japan
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract Test results are presented on a new brake system which maintains high performance even under adverse weather conditions, and on its new brake-control-method to achieve Wheel Slide-Protection. This system is characterized by its control associated with the saturation point of the creep-region (micro-slide), sensitive slide-detection, application of alloyed-castiron brake-shoes to tread-brakes for high speed tilting-trains or disk-brakes for high speed Shinkansen, testing and analysis apparatus, and Multi-Mode brake WSP control-method and new Individual-Continuous brake-control system by Electro-Pneumatic change valve of Normal-Open type. The new brake system is capable of maintaining high adhesion even under adverse conditions of snowy or icy rails. The actual-average braking-deceleration of new brake system from the initial-speed of 140 km/h under adverse condition was 5.4 km/h/s, one of the highest among high-speed trains in the absence of rail-brakes. The shortened travel time has made it possible to discontinue competition of rail with airlines between Sapporo and Hakodate, showing that the increased deceleration and train speed can be instrumental in attracting more passengers. And the new brake system is superior in cost, weight, reliability, maintenance, and performance too. Therefore, it is expected to promote speedup and reduce hcadways and also to reduce total cost for Future Railways.

Title ABB onboard energy storage system as an upgrade solution for DMUs and catenary-free tram operation
 
Author Parvizi, A
Affiliation ABB Australia
Source Informa
Conference AusRAIL PLUS 2019, Delivering growth; creating opportunity; embracing technology, 3-5 December 2019, Sydney, NSW, Australia
Publication Date 2019-12
Abstract A complete Energy Storage System (ESS) as a reliable technology and technical solution to upgrade the existing rolling stocks, i.e. Diesel Multiple Units (DMUs) and Light Rail Vehicles (LRVs) is presented in this paper by which up to 30 percent energy saving could be achieved in rail transportation. Energy storage solutions are now offering the advantages of regenerative braking also on non-electrified tracks, which helps to avoid costly investment into catenary infrastructure. In a diesel hybrid solution, the energy from regenerative braking is used to charge the battery that can be used in parallel with the diesel engine as a booster to save fuel as well as supplying auxiliary loads at the train platform to reduce local noise emission and air pollution. Another application is the installation of an ESS to enable catenary-free operation of EMUs or trams. These two applications of the energy storage system for DMUs and LRVs are presented in technical studies and the methodology to execute the project is reviewed.

Title Amtrak's high speed trainset program
 
Author Bell, JG
Annechini, RJ
Green, JR
Affiliation High Speed Trainset Program Amtrak, Philadelphia, PA, USA
LTK Engineering Services, Philadelphia, PA, USA
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract On Amtrak's 25th Anniversary, May 1, 1996, Amtrak signed a contract with a Consortium consisting of Bombardier and GEC Aisthom for 12 high speed electric Trainsets (and an option for another 6), 15 high speed electric locomotives, two maintenance facilities and provision of maintenance services. The high speed Trainsets will operate at speeds up to 150 mph (240 km/h). The Trainsets will incorporate many characteristics of the Swedish X2000 and German ICE, tested by Amtrak in 1993, as well as the TGV and other state-of-the art Trainsets in worldwide operation. The lightweight Trainsets will have an A.C. propulsion system, and be built to Amtrak's new structural standards. These standards have never been used in the domestic passenger rail industry, and will provide the greatest amount of crew and passenger safety possible within the limits of current technology. The electric Trainsets will reduce New York to Washington trip times to two hours and fifteen minutes. Between Boston and Washington, these Trainsets, along with right-of-way improvements, will dramatically increase market share by reducing travel time from nearly five hours to under three. The Trainsets will go through the most extensive commissioning test in Amtrak's history. During Trainset construction, the Association of American Railroads' Transportation Technology Center will be upgraded for this program. Tests will be performed at speeds up to 165 mph (265 km/h) and up to 12 inches (300 mm) of cant deficiency. Additional tests will be performed on Amtrak's Northeast Corridor.

Affiliation Pandrol Rail Fastenings Ltd., Surrey, UK
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract The paper addresses the issue of whether the·current design considerations for fastening systems, which are usually based on the condition of low speed traffic running at cant excess, need to be reconsidered as a result of the introduction of higher speed ,trains, including tilting trains, which operate at high cant deficiencies. The results of measurements of fastening system deflections on three different curves, with two types of railpads, two track gauges, and under two types of tilting train are discussed. When the results of these measurements are normalised so that they can meaningfully be compared with each other, it can be seen that some general conclusions can be drawn. Even when the measured results are extrapolated to allow for high levels of cant deficiency, the most demanding condition for the fastening system remains that of slower trains running at cant excess.The trends found from the measurements are supported by predictions of fastening deflection made with a non-linear track model, using load inputs which have in tum been calculated from a model of vehicle curving behaviour.

Title Recent investigations on track lateral shift limits for high-speed rail applications
 
Author Kish, A
Samavedam, G
Wormley, D
Affiliation USDOT /Volpe Center, Cambridge, MA, USA
Foster-Miller, Inc., Waltham, MA, USA
Penn State University, University Park, PA, USA
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract As part of the USDOT's initiatives in high-speed rail applications, safety standard are being developed by the Federal Railroad Administration (FRA). This paper deals with a part of this research addressing the development of criteria and safety limits for track lateral shift mitigation. Specifically, the fundamental mechanics of track lateral shift due to vehicle and thermally induced loads are defined, and an analytic approach is presented for the determination of limit loads and deflections on the track to prevent progressive lateral shift. The proposed approach consists of a tandem use of a comprehensive vehicle-track dynamics model and a track shift model. The vehicle-track dynamic model predicts lateral and vertical wheel-rail interaction loads, taking into account the effect of track compliance. The lateral shift model predicts the cumulative lateral deflections under multiple vehicle passes. Results of baseline parametric studies accounting for the influences of the lateral resistance parameters, track curvature, rail longitudinal force, and other vehicle and track parameters are given, and prototype track lateral shift mitigation criteria are proposed. The model results are correlated with recent SNCF test data on high-speed tracks.

Title Calculation procedures for the electromagnetic design of high-speed maglev systems
 
Author Andriollo, M
Martinelli, G
Morini, A
Tortella, A
Affiliation University of Padova, Italy
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract The high-speed magnetically levitated transport systems (MAGLEV) of both electrodynamic (EDS) and electromagnetic (EMS) type have the levitation and propulsion devices integrated in the structure of a linear synchronous motor (LSM). The electromagnetic design of the systems requires a detailed analysis which cannot be accurately performed on the basis of the established theory of electrical machines, due to the unconventional configurations. Therefore, advanced numerical codes and powerful means of calculations are generally required for the electromagnetic analysis, in particular if an optimization has to be undertaken. The paper describes calculation procedures developed with the aim to get a set of efficient calculation tools expressly created for the electromagnetic design of both EDS and EMS MAGLEV systems, with good performance even on personal computers and with reduced operator supervision. The procedures allow to perform; the evaluation of the map of the magnetic field; the analysis of the electromagnetic interaction between the propulsion and levitation subsystems; the evaluation of the electromagnetic quantities (currents, emfs, voltages, forces and powers) defining the system performance; the analysis of the contactless electrical power transfer from the on-ground windings to the on-hoard linear generator (LG) which supplies the on-board loads; the simulation of electrical and mechanical transients; the optimization of the performance of a specific subsystem and/or the optimization of the global performances of the whole electromagnetic system. As examples of application, the paper presents some results among those obtained by applying the proposed procedures to EDS and EMS configurations with reference to the JR [1,2] and Transrapid prototypes

Abstract One of the most important and difficult problem of the high speed train is "car-body vibration". Also if the radius of curve is small, lateral regular acceleration becomes a large problem. In order to conquer these problems, the 300X Shinkansen experimental train set has many innovative methods. In this paper, we will introduce 4 methods as follows. Dampers between cars: in order to reduce lateral vibration of car-body, the 300X train set has 4 longitudinal dampers and 2 lateral dampers between adjacent cars. The system is composed of hydraulic cylinders under the 2nd spring, hydraulic pressure equipment, and controllers. Abstract Running resistance comprises aerodynamic and mechanical drag. For high-speed trains, such as Shinkansen trains, and conventional trains, the running resistance is dominated by the aerodynamic drag because of their increasing speeds and lightening weights. Accordingly, reducing the aerodynamic drag of the trains has become a viable option for reducing their energy consumption. In this study, we evaluated the effect of improving train shapes on aerodynamic drag reduction for Shinkansen and conventional trains by means of wind tunnel tests. Furthermore, for conventional trains, we estimated the energy-saving effects of decreased aerodynamic drag by using a train simulator. To reduce aerodynamic drag of the Shinkansen trains, we proposed practical under-bogie covers. As for the conventional trains, we proposed modified shapes of rooftop and underfloor equipments, and decreased the aerodynamic drag of the conventioal train set composed of eight-vehicles by 22%.

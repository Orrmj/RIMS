Title An innovative approach to metropolitan train carriage interior configuration; to improve boarding, alighting, dwell time stability and passenger experience
 
Author Coxon, S
Sarvi, M
Napper, R
Affiliation Monash University, Clayton, Victoria, Australia
Conference World Conference on Transport Research, 13th, 2013, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
Publication Date 2013-07
Abstract Suburban rail operators in Australia, as in many other countries, are facing considerable challenges in the rapid growth of patronage. Higher passenger densities particularly during peak times of the day have implications upon train punctuality, crowding, accessibility and passenger comfort. There are a number of alternative strategies available to transport operating companies; ticket pricing policy, increasing services, lengthening platforms and track amplification. Some research suggests that the design of the train carriage interior has a significant influence upon accessibility and passenger dispersal once on-board. This paper describes research into the re-configuration of metro carriage interiors for the Melbourne rail network that seeks to ameliorate problems of doorway occlusion and passenger dispersal with the aim of stabilising dwell times and improving passenger experience. The data for this study was drawn from the Melbourne suburban rail network due to its particular challenges in accommodating both a metro (short trip) system with a lengthy outer suburban network on tracks shared with regional and freight services. The research methodology embraced two strategies; An Industrial Design studio methodology that combined the collation of factual evidence to inform an empirical design activity consisting of drawings and models. The study then embraced computational simulations to determine the efficacy of a developed design concept. The result of the this process was a design that embraced flexible seating; extra doors that operated only during peak periods without sacrificing seating and strategies that manipulated passenger flow. Subsequent computational modelling of this concept indicated that these innovations can improve dwell time stability and passenger dispersal for the Melbourne rail network. The work also demonstrates to a wider audience that it is possible to explore the design of carriage interiors to improve network performance as an alternative to current costly operating strategies.

Title Passenger demand-based train regulation for CBTC
 
Author Leveque, O
Affiliation Alstom - Australia New-Zealand
Source Informa
Conference AusRAIL PLUS 2019, Delivering growth; creating opportunity; embracing technology, 3-5 December 2019, Sydney, NSW, Australia
Publication Date 2019-12
Abstract Traffic regulation aims to support the performance of the transport system in efficiently and reliably satisfying the service requirements and passenger experience. Current transport systems utilise a theoretical timetable which includes operational margins allowing the mitigation and recovery of some delays. But theoretical timetables are designed based on an average estimate of the passenger demand which cannot accurately reflect the reality of the passenger density on platforms over time along a line. As a matter of fact, traditional traffic regulation systems do not allow for any adjustments of the timetable in real-time with the actual passenger demand at stations but only regulate station dwell-times and travel times to recover the evaluated delay according to the target schedule or headway depending on the regulation strategy in use. This paper presents an innovative automatic train regulation solution for CBTC systems, which provides a real-time regulation according to the actual density of passengers waiting on platforms while at the same time controlling train departure and arrival times to avoid resulting delays. The proposed solution offers an efficient way to optimize the transport offer through significant enhancements of the service capacity, operational fluidity and reliability without major upgrades of the existing infrastructure.

Title A guiding system for the persons handicapped to move
 
Author Goto, K
Matsubara, H
Myojo, S
Abe, Y
Tanaka, M
Affiliation Railway Technical Research Institute, Tokyo, Japan
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract Railway Technical Research Institute(RTRI) is now conducting research and development on various measures to enhance safety and increase serviceability of railway passengers. Especially, an experimental measure for persons visually handicapped is now under development. Recently various guiding systems for such a purpose have been developed. However information provided by those existing systems is not sufficient and not necessarily appropriate to each user, mainly because information is conveyed through voice via a speaker installed on the ground or a portable radio carried by individual users. And generally, such a system requires vast investment in order to install facilities in large area. The system proposed by RTRI utilizes the latest technology of data-carrier, mobile communication and portable computers. Data-carriers are installed in the ground, on the platform, in the wall, and so on. Coded information of data-carriers is transferred to a visually handicapped user via a data-carrier reader which is installed within a cane by the user. This information is interpreted by the portable computer, such as PDA (Personal Digital Assistant), and then the PDA generates an appropriate guiding message referring to the user's personal data inputted beforehand, such as the user's destination, the state of handicap, or the language which the user understands, etc. Finally this guiding message is conveyed to the user by voice via a portable speaker.

Title A new architecture for the regional trains
 
Author Cortese, V
Palloni, G
Affiliation Politecnico di Torino, Torino, Italy
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract In this paper the Authors present a new train for regional railway service. Here reported are the results of a large study focused on the weight reduction of a railway passenger car: this problem is approached in a new way, refeITing to a double-deck composite materials structure, adopting a new solution for its general architecture and configuration. Key aspects of the research are the main criteria of a new coach-project with the analyses of possible and existing train configurations. The main problems are: structure weight reduction; passenger comfort increase; train reliability improvement. The train here proposed is called TRIS-BP (Trena Innovativo Suburbano BiPiano: Double-Deck Suburban Innovating Train) and it is proposed for railway services in the main area of the largest towns and for the regional rapid transport. It is an electric traction block train based on a modular concept. The basic configuration is variable to support specific traffic requirements, allowing some simplifications in the research and development phase. If compared to the classic solutions, this one permits higher performances, thanks to its lower weight. To get the shortening of the stopping time the passenger entrance level is at the standard platform level (600 mm over rail level). Furthermore air conditioning equipment and a large number of comfortable seats are provided for passenger confort. New developments of this train are now estimated, aiming at the study of new light weight bogies and other improvements for passenger comfort. In a word the TRIS-BP is a train that joins the advantages of a conventional push-pull train to the others of an electric traction self-propelled one, thanks to its high performances and reliability.

Title Methodology to predict and to optimize the dynamic behaviour of railway waggons based on combined numerical and experimental simulations
 
Author Wandinger, J
Grillenbeck, A
Dillinger, S
Diekhoff, H-J
Villmann, J
Affiliation IABG mbH, Ottobrunn, Germany
Deutsche Bohn AG, Minden, Germany
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract In recent years the dynamic investigation of railway vehicles has gained more and more of importance. In view of the passengers' demand for more comfort, the Deutsche Bahn AG as well as the vehicle industry currently look for methodologies to predict the dynamic behaviour and to evaluate alternative solutions in a very early design stage. Numerical methods provide an invaluable tool to meet this task. A new carriage design, for instance, can be optimized before the first prototype exists and parametric studies can be performed to understand the physics involved in all dynamic phenomena. However, a complete carriage is a rather complex and also complicated mechanical system. Therefore, its numerical model has to be assembled step by step and accompanying measurements should be performed whenever possible in order to validate the assumptions and simplifications of the theoretical model. The paper reports on the development of a general methodology to predict the dynamic and acoustic behaviour of carriages in order to assess the passengers' comfort. As realistic example, a complete ICE 1 carriage has been modelled with Finite Elements. Special solution algorithms and reduction schemes have been implemented to keep the size of the model in acceptable bounds. In order to check the quality of the simulation model, extensive experimental investigations have been carried out. These included the measurement of frequency response functions to determine the modal parameters of the complete ICE carriage as well as the acquisition of some acoustic data which will be used in future investigations. The comparison of numerical and measured results is discussed and the methodology critically reviewed.

Abstract As the transport industry is challenged by the need for increased passenger capacity, travelling in a standing position is becoming more common. To maintain a competitive advantage, transport operators and manufacturers will wish to ensure that this evolution does not happen to the detriment of the comfort of passengers. Title Holistic connection dispatching: approach and prototype
 
Author Oetting, A
Stelzer, A
Affiliation Railway Engineering, Technische Universitä Darmstadt, Darmstadt, Germany
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract Connection Dispatching gains more and more attention in fast developing multimodal passenger traffic systems with a growing complexity. Therefore an IT-based system needs to be developed which supports the connection dispatcher in his objective – to deal with connection conflicts in such a way the traveller is able to arrive at his planned destination. As this cannot be achieved at any price, a cost evaluation for the results of the dispatching process is necessary. This englobes, for des-integrated European railway companies, both cost of infrastructure and of train operating companies. The system described in this paper consists of several modules, including modules for conflict detection, conflict visualization, conflict evaluation, conflict resolution, evaluation of conflict resolution measures and a module responsible for combining different applicable resolution measures. Abstract The research program is carried out to design, prototype and experiment new decision support functions dedicated to the traffic management. In a final step, the objective is to connect these functions with the control and command layer, especially for route programming and transmission of speed or schedule instructions to trains. A new concept which is supported and modelled through the research program is the forecast train traffic plan. It updates the train traffic plan with the confirmed traffic management decisions and their foreseen consequences. This paper gives an overview of the target decision support system subject of the SNCF research program.

Abstract The dispatching of a large area in a railway net is a very challenging task because of the many constraints that have to be taken into account during the decision making process. Nowadays, dispatching is mainly taken care of by human operators. None of the algorithmic approaches attempted in the last three decades could solve this task. Their computational complexity or simulation accuracy was insufficient for practical application. The dispatching method presented in this work brings a new algorithmic approach with practical application. Based on the optimisation method specially developed for this purpose, optimal dispatching decisions are calculated. The resulting conflict-free timetable can be directly implemented in the railway operation. Additionally, the resulting timetable represents an input for the energy saving train speed control.

Title Railway traffic optimization by advanced scheduling and rerouting algorithms

Author D'Ariano, A

Affiliation Department of Transport & Planning, Delft University of Technology, Delft, The Netherlands
Conference 8th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 18-22 2008
Publication Date 2008
Abstract In the last years, real-time railway traffic optimization experienced an increasing interest due to the expected growth of traffic and the limited possibilities of enhancing the infrastructure, which ask for a more efficient use of resources and the application of more advanced decision support tools. This paper presents a computerized train dispatching system, called ROMA (Railway traffic Optimization by Means of Alternative graphs), for supporting railway traffic controllers during operations. Innovative scheduling and rerouting algorithms have been developed in order to globally optimize disturbed railway traffic conditions. ROMA can anticipate the evolution of traffic, including the propagation of delays in a regional railway network, and can estimate the effects of different dispatching measures during a period of about 15 minutes ahead. Therefore, ROMA would enable traffic controllers to frequently perform incremental changes to the actual timetable to accommodate changes in traffic patterns due to disturbances, such as train delays and blocked tracks. An extensive computational study is carried out, based on a dispatching area of the Dutch railway network, to show the high potential of our train dispatching system as a support tool to improve punctuality.

Title Broadband wireless connectivity for high speed trains: towards the internet of things in rail
 
Author Erta, A
Bisti, L
Lionetti, G
Malesci, U
Pozzi, DE
Affiliation Fluidmesh Networks, Milan, Italy
Sirti SPA, Milan, Italy
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract Providing broadband access to a high speed train is extremely challenging. This paper describes Fluidity, Fluidmesh Networks mobility solution specifically designed for train-to-ground broadband communication up to 400 Km/h using the unlicensed 5 GHz frequency band. Fluidity is a micro-mobility solution based on an MPLS core that guarantees seamless handoff and uninterrupted train-to-trackside communication through a make-before-break approach. Fluidity performance has been evaluated in a real-life pilot test-bed consisting of equipping sixteen pre-existing GSM-R trackside masts covering roughly 70 Km of high speed commercial rail track between Milan and Turin, in Italy. The test-bed has been designed and installed by Sirti in a short timespan in collaboration with Rete Ferroviaria Italiana (RFI) and Trenitalia. A high-speed test train has been equipped with Fluidmesh proprietary rooftop antennas and radios to conduct the test. This paper presents a summary of the results achieved during the eleven round trip runs occurring between November and December 2015. Fluidity proved to be a suitable train-to-ground solution for a variety of applications, including onboard Wi-Fi and diagnostic operations offering peak throughput in excess of 100 Mb/s (TCP). The end-to-end average throughput exceeded 35 Mb/s with the average computed including areas with lack of radio coverage due physical obstacles such as highway bridges and overpasses. Nonetheless, the technology proved to be flexible in increasing the performance by simply installing additional antennas in pre-existing shelters and buildings along the trackside boosting the performance by 50% in such areas with no additional civil work required. Finally, Fluidity proved to be well suited for tunnel operations, both in terms of signal level and throughput reaching 58 Mb/s average TCP throughput along the entire 1.7 Km of the tunnel which was part of the test-bed.

Title Use of artificial vision in vehicle monitoring
 
Author Fry, K
Affiliation BRR, Derby, UK
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract Artificial vision systems offer a number of possibilities for automating rail vehicle maintenance inspection and optimising the use of consumables. The inspection of brake pad condition is an important area, affecting high consumable costs and determining the most frequent examination interval. Automatic brake pad measurement therefore represents an ideal area for the application of artificial vision in vehicle monitoring. In recent years, BRR, part of AEA Technology, has developed an automatic system for the measurement of brake pad thickness. The system, called PadVIEWÂ®, allows a new approach to pad maintenance, resulting in significant cost savings. PadVIEWÂ® measures the thickness of each pad in a train as it passes over a trackside system. These measurements are then down loaded to a database on a depot Information Display System. From the measurements in its database PadVIEWÂ® calculates the wear rate and remaining life of each pad. Given the maximum interval between exams, the system recommends which pads should be changed in order to let the vehicle run until its next exam. PadVIEWÂ® allows pad changes to be based upon the individual wear rates of each pad, freeing pad maintenance from the large variations that exist between pad positions. The material on each pad is used to the maximum, within the limits set by the pad's own wear rate and the vehicle's exam frequency giving significant savings in pad material. The system also helps with brake system fault diagnosis by identifying faulty actuators, sticking callipers and dragging brakes. As a quality control tool the system provides checks on pad suppliers and allows easy service trials of alternative components.

Abstract Most of the pantographs are monitored through periodic, preventive maintenance. The check for the health status can be slow, expensive and hazardous. Moreover, the process to detect defects and potentially dangerous conditions involves a degree of human judgment [1]. Off the shelf systems for an automated in-line inspection of pantographs become more and more popular in the past years, it is reasonable to think about a spreading of these solutions in the near future [2]. At this time, those criteria could be defined by pantographs producers, rolling stock management, railway network management, typically without any supervising entity, nor international standard guidelines. An approach to process the above mentioned data in a model independent way is proposed, exploiting properties that should be valid for all different pantographs models such as smoothness of the top surface, symmetry (for deformations check), rotations, parallelisms. Present analysis proposes most appropriate parameters, the way to measure them, the suggested ranges for classifying the status of the inspected pantograph. This can be also considered as a guideline to open a discussion aimed to the definition of an international standard.

Ideally suited to both green and brown field rollouts, an IP/MPLS based network is a viable solution for any network rollout.

Title Study on train control technology in virtual coupling concept

Publication Date 2019
Abstract In recent years, 'Virtual Coupling' or named 'Closer Running' has been researching in international research agendas such as European Shift2Rail research initiative and the UK Railway Technical Strategy. In virtual coupling concept, trains could be virtually coupled by radio communication when moving, instead of any physical connection at standstill. When trains virtual coupled, they can runs much closer each other and the headway will be within their absolute braking distance, which brings challenge to conventional signal system. Generally, it is believed that headway between virtually-coupled trains should be controlled automatically by some equipment instead of drivers, such as signal system or rolling stock. This paper aims to propose the realization of train control technology for âvirtually-coupled trainsâ in signal system. According to whether train headway control is protected by signal system and mode of on-board equipment, three schemes are put forward in this paper, and their basic principles and modification of current ERTMS/ETCS specifications are described. In the scheme based on relative braking distance, one algorithm of calculating braking curves is put forward. Finally, the paper analyses the challenges faced by virtual coupling in high-speed railway and some possible advantages in urban rail transit. The author thinks that virtual coupling technology may be firstly applied in urban rail traffic.

Abstract In the Tokyo metropolitan area, the night interval time between train operations is very short, so in order switching from the old to new train control system, It is difficult to secure online examination time before switching. ATC (Automatic Train Control) is a system that realizes train detection and data transmission from wayside equipment to on-board equipment using rail as a medium. If we can coexist the old/new systems so as not to electrically influence on the rail, it is possible to conduct sufficient online test during the train operation time. In order to coexist with old/new systems, it is necessary to solve various railway specific technical issues, such as electrical interference between old/new systems and elimination of noise. In the past, after adopting a desk study, we adopted a method of trying to solve technical problems while carrying out online-field tests of prototypes. However, this method requires cycles of âspecification change -> design -> retestâ every time a problem occurs, so there was a problem in cost and construction period. Therefore, by using STAMP/STPA (System Theoretic Accident Model and Processes/System Theoretic Process Analysis) which is a new safety analysis method different from the conventional FTA/FMEA, we tried to evaluate the mutual influence of coexistence of old/new systems.

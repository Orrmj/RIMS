Abstract It has been made clear that acoustic-absorptive materials (recycled) are effective for reducing the wheel/rail noise on slab tracks. They are products of crushed glass and ceramics. They are produced from household trash, but are devoid of injurious elements such as metals and salt. Some devices are needed to hold them in place without being blown away by train passeage. To confirm their noise reducing effect, they were applied on paved track of existing line, and the noise level was measured. The noise level decreased approximately 3dB(A)at 2m away from track center. Further, a test was done to confirm their noise-reducing effect in comparison with a sound protection wall.

Abstract A wheel profile of a heavy haul train is usually optimised to perform best in nominal operational conditions i.e. standard track gauge, limited cant deficiency, nominal rail profiles etc. However, it is impossible to avoid changes in the wheel-rail friction level, cant-deficiency, track gauge widening in tight curves, etc. These phenomena significantly affect the wheel-rail contact conditions which results in changing the wheel and rail profile shapes. The change of wheel profile alters the contact conditions and in turn the stress levels at the wheel surface which shortens the wheel life. By using multibody dynamic simulations as a virtual-twin to the real operation of the vehicle on track, it is possible to predict the running distance between two consecutive re-profiling actions due to wear and/or rolling contact fatigue. Thus, by changing the operational conditions i.e. speed, axle load, traction etc., the changes in the wheel life can be monitored. This tool helps the operators to optimise the operational conditions and plan the track and wheel maintenance to avoid excessive damage. As an example, a verified model of the LKAB Iron-ore locomotive is chosen for the study. The results for the normal operational case are compared with the available field observations and very good agreement is achieved. The study is repeated for various cases and the results are discussed.

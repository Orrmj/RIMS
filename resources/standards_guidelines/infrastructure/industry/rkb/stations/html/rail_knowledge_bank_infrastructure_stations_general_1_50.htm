Title Research on the method of capacity assessment of heavy-haul railway
 
Author Ma, L
Deng, J
Yuan, L
Ding, R
Affiliation Beijing Jiaotong University, Beijing, China
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th International Heavy Haul Conference, (IHHA 2019), Achieving breakthrough performance levels, 12-14 June, 2019, Narvik, Norway
Publication Date 2019
Abstract To update the signal system to moving block is one of the efficient methods for the improvement of heavy-haul railways’ capacity. The quantitative assessment for the capacity is concerned by railway administration when moving block system is applied. This paper presents a method which can assess the arrival/departure capacity of heavy-haul railway stations in moving block system. First, a breadth-first search algorithm based on colon-graph topology structure was applied to search all routes and locate the bottleneck serial route nodes (SRN) on which maximum headway will be obtained. Then, the headway of different types of trains in the bottleneck route was calculated and the response time of relative signal equipment and the control model of Automatic Train Protection (ATP) system were taken into account in the calculation. At last, based on the above calculation, the worst train type that results in maximum headway was found out. The average headway considering all possible traffic diagrams could be calculated further when the maximum headway is greater than expected. The case study based on Shuohuang heavy-haul railway in China showed that this method can be used in the assessment of capacity and improved results were contrasted to reach expected headway in moving block.

Title A high-efficiency method of boarding and alighting for passengers in intercity railway transport of China
 
Author Lv, X
Li, J
Duan, J
Li, J
Wang, Z
Affiliation Institute of Computing Technology, China Academy of Railway Sciences, Beijing 100081, China
China Academy of Railway Sciences, Beijing 100081, China
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract The intercity railway travel is attracting more and more passengers with its advantages of safety, environmental and fastness, which alleviates the traffic pressure on the intercity road transport to a large extent. However, there exist some aspects can be improved for the operations of intercity railway transport in China compared with other developed countries. Currently, the boarding and alighting of intercity railway transport for passengers utilize the following way in China, checking tickets at the station entrance, the entrance to platform and the station exit in turn, which consumes huge amounts of manpower and material resources due to the tedious examinations of machine or artificiality. Meanwhile, the tedious examinations lead to a low efficiency and plenty of passengers staying in the station. To improve the efficiency of boarding and alighting for intercity railway transport of China, a new method used to passengers’ boarding and alighting is presented in this paper. In the proposed method, the duty of station is no longer checking tickets for passengers but guides the passengers to board the trains by determining the current number of remaining seats on the train based on the described terminal APP. The process of checking tickets is completed through the self-serve checking on the described on-board ticket examiner when the passengers enter into the carriages. Then the passengers who have completed verification select the desired seats via the on-board ticket examiner or terminal APP. At last, the train attendants only check the identity information of the passengers who have not seat or sit on the seats that should be vacant by the terminal APP. The proposed method for passengers’ boarding and alighting avoids the tedious process of checking tickets in the station and can quickly guides the passengers access to carriages or leave the station, thus the transport efficiency for passengers in the intercity railways is improved. Meanwhile, the proposed method allows the passengers who have the boarding certificate to board the trains at any time during the operation of the trains.

Title Assessing practical capacity utilisation limits at railway stations and junctions
 
Author Preston, J
Armstrong, J
Affiliation University of Southampton, Southampton, UK
Arup, London, UK
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract As traffic levels increase on the world’s railways, improved understanding of the trade-offs between capacity provision/utilisation and service quality is increasingly important, as Infrastructure Managers (IMs) and Railway Undertakings (RUs) seek to maximise capacity provision without an unacceptable loss of service reliability and punctuality. This is particularly true of the stations and junctions forming the nodes of railway networks, as they tend to be the capacity bottlenecks, and the relationships between capacity utilisation and performance are less well understood for nodes than for their intermediate links. Following on from initial work undertaken for the OCCASION project on the calculation of nodal Capacity Utilisation Indices (CUIs), this paper briefly describes the application of the techniques developed for OCCASION to the recalibration of the Capacity Charge element of the Track Access Charges on Britain’s railways. The Capacity Charge varies by time and location in accordance with traffic intensity, and is designed to encourage RUs to make efficient use of the network; its recalibration was based upon the network-wide relationship between link-based CUIs and Congestion-Related Reactionary Delay (CRRD). The paper then describes research being undertaken for the DITTO Rail Systems project to build upon the OCCASION and Capacity Charge Recalibration (CCR) work to develop more generalised capacity utilisation measures for junctions and stations and, importantly, to identify practical upper limits for capacity utilisation, beyond which performance is likely to deteriorate to an unacceptable degree.

Title Capacity at railway stations
 
Subtitle Meeting the challenges for future mobility: proceedings
Author Landex, A
Affiliation Technical University of Denmark, Kgs. Lyngby, Denmark
Conference 9th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 22-26 2011, Lille, France
Publication Date 2011
Abstract Stations do have other challenges regarding capacity than open lines as it is here the traffic is dispatched. The UIC 406 capacity method that can be used to analyse the capacity consumption can be exposed in different ways at stations which may lead to different results. Therefore, stations need special focus when conducting UIC 406 capacity analyses. This paper describes how the UIC 406 capacity method can be expounded for stations. Commonly for the analyses of stations it is recommended to include the entire station including the switch zone(s) and all station tracks. By including the switch zone(s) the possible conflicts with other trains (also in the opposite direction) are taken into account, leading to more trustworthy results. Although the UIC 406 methodology proposes that the railway network should be divided into line sections when trains turn around and when the train order is changed, this paper recommends that the railway lines are not always be divided. In case trains turn around on open (single track) line, the capacity consumption may be too low if a railway line is divided. The same can be the case if only few trains are overtaken at an overtaking station. For dead end stations and overtaking stations, the dwell/layover time is recommended to be reduced to the minimum required time as it results in the lowest possible capacity consumption. For dead end stations, it is furthermore recommended that the trains can use all possible tracks and not only those tracks they were originally assigned. For complex stations with shunting movement, the results of UIC 406 capacity analyses are imprecise due to different possible routes and no exact knowledge of shunting movements. For these stations it is instead recommended that they are analysed with a supplement to compensate for the inaccuracies.

Author Loum, D

Affiliation Université de Lille Nord de France, Lille, France

Conference 9th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 22-26 2011, Lille, France
Publication Date 2011
Abstract Nowadays, because of its wide bandwidth and high communication capability, optical fiber is more and more used for high data rate transmission of information in railway environments. Conventionally, only one service is sent over the fiber at a time. However, many different services can be simultaneously conveyed in railway stations such as passenger information service, cellular phone, Wi-Fi... The objective of the work proposed in this paper is to demonstrate the potential benefits of transmitting radio signals over fiber in a railway environment.

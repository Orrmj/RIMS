Title Steel bridge fatigue life estimation using probabilistic method
 
Author Rakoczy, AM
Otter, D
Dick, S
Affiliation Transportation Technology Center, Inc. (TTCI), Pueblo CO, USA
Purdue University Bowen Laboratory, West Lafayette IN, USA
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th International Heavy Haul Conference, (IHHA 2019), Achieving breakthrough performance levels, 12-14 June, 2019, Narvik, Norway
Publication Date 2019
Abstract Existing fatigue life evaluation procedures are often over-simplified and do not account accurately for the uncertainty in applied load and fatigue resistance of steel railway bridges. This paper focuses on development of probabilistic, risk-based methods and models for assessing steel bridge Fitness for Service (FFS) and remaining life. The models developed in this project are calibrated, validated, and verified against all available and relevant fatigue test results. For some bridge types, these models provide remaining fatigue life estimates that are double and sometimes even triple those obtained by using existing simplified methods. One potential consequence of using simplified approaches is that bridges that are not in need of renewal might appear as a higher priority than bridges with a shorter remaining life. The FFS methods explored also provide information about the expected life of bridges that can be used to establish improved bridge inspection programs.

Title Heavy haul train dominant frequencies: insights and practical use
 
Author Busatta, F
Moyo, P
Affiliation University of Cape Town, Rondebosch, South Africa
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th International Heavy Haul Conference, (IHHA 2019), Achieving breakthrough performance levels, 12-14 June, 2019, Narvik, Norway
Publication Date 2019
Abstract Excitation frequencies are generally imposed on railway bridges due to the repetitive moving loads generated by a sequence of rail cars, with regular wheelset distances, crossing the deck. The resulting loading spectrum usually depends on a combination of parameters such as train speed, axle load, train-set, number and spacing of axles. In this paper, the excitation induced by heavy haul trains is investigated in terms of train dominant frequencies experimentally estimated from Wheel-Impact-Monitoring Weigh-In-Motion big data collected on the Iron Ore Export Line in South Africa. Through automated analysis of several thousand trains, it is shown that these trains are characterised by time-varying speed while crossing bridges. In addition, clarification is provided on the role played by the parameters mentioned above which might act as frequency or amplitude modulators, thereby shaping the loading spectrum of each crossing differently. Ultimately, the practical use of the train dominant frequencies is discussed. It is shown that the loading spectra might be used to judge the train-set and rolling stock used currently, to design new wagons, and define guidelines on the rail operating conditions in crossing railway bridges in order to preserve their structural integrity.

Title Transition structures of railway bridges
 
Author Smekal, A
Affiliation Swedish National Rail Administration, Borlange, Sweden
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract Anyone travelling in Europe by train is aware of different quality of track on bridges and adjacent embankments. Problems with transition structures of railway bridges are well known and they are a source of increased maintenance costs and can be the reason for reduced track availability. Maintenance staff regularly reports about a phenomenon called disappearance of ballast. A difference in stiffness between relatively soft embankment and very stiff bridge, usually founded without allowing any additional settlement, creates a bump at the end of the bridge which always cause problems under cyclic and dynamic train loading. There are many reasons for substantial increase of maintenance required in the transition area, such as settlements of an inadequately compacted backfilling, settlements of adjacent embankment, repeated displacements of the track or bridge under the load or due to changes in temperature. All the settlements, displacements and movements described above increase in transition area of railway bridges with the time and due to dynamic and cyclic loading. Deterioration of ballast is an another noticeable problem close to the bridge heads. All sources of deformation cause a deterioration of track geometry in vertical and horizontal direction. Though these are familiar problems, a few studies have been carried out to find better solutions for transition structures. It is necessary to find such solutions in a joint effort involving four fields of civil engineering in area of track, bridge, geotechnical and maintenance engineering. Solutions should be sought for existing bridges to establish conditions for upgrading of the transition area without disturbing the present track availability. At the same time designs for transition structures for new bridges should be presented, the solutions that substantially decrease the cost of the future maintenance.

Abstract As the rapid development of domestic economy in China, there is an urgent and necessary demand for the construction of new heavy-haul railways and the updating of existing freight capacity of railway. As for the railway infrastructures, the reconstruction or reinforcement of the existing bridges is the key in terms of economy, construction time and safety. The heavier freight loads require higher bearing capacities, which means the design strength of the existing bridges is too low. On the other hand, the strength and the durability of the existing bridges get weakened due to the damages as the increase of service time. It is of vital importance to develop a feasible and economic reinforcement system used for the existing bridges. The strengthening system with externally prestressed CFRP (Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer) plates is attractive due to its definite force transfer, distinct strengthening effect, low stress around anchorage zone, no disturbance to operation, and little damage to original structure. Unfortunately, it is very difficult for common anchors to exert a high strength on CFRP plates, which limited its extensive application in engineering both home and abroad. The wedge-shaped anchor introduced in this paper had solved this technical issue â reliable anchorage of highly prestressed CFRP plates. The carbon fiber plate assembled with this wedge-shaped anchor can be installed easily. The size of the whole assembly system is small. In a series of experimental test, the static tensile strength, relaxation and fatigue behavior of CFRP plates were studied. The tensile strength of plates was over 2500MPa. When the prestressing strength reached 1700MPa, the assembly system still indicated low relaxation and good fatigue behavior. This new reinforcement system with externally prestressed CFRP plates has been used in the in-service bridges in freight railways. Due to its efficiency and reliability, it will still be extensively used for the reinforcement of the existing bridges.

AVLS, Orsay, France
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract Dynamics of railway bridges is a sensitive topic as it concerns traffic running safety and maintenance optimisation. Important vibrations of the railway bridge decks can destabilise the ballast and degrade the contact between the train wheels and the rails. If these vibrations are not controlled, it can imply increased maintenance needs, and if the degradation is too fast, a higher risk of train derailment and track buckling at hot temperature. For most of the bridges with a span length more than 10m, simple and fast calculation techniques have proved to be relevant to model these phenomena. Models such as simple supported beams with the passage of point moving forces to represent the train axle loads were compared to measurements on real bridges and were found to be close. However, for short-span bridges, these techniques are not valid anymore because they highly overestimate the results compared to reality. It has been shown that for these bridges, the shell effects, the influence of a possible skew, of the track, of the bearings and the interaction between the vehicle and the bridge have to be taken into account in order to better model the real movement of the bridge.

Abstract Strengthening by external cables is often used when the load-carrying capacity of a prestressed concrete bridge declines. However, in the case of PC-I girder bridges with the narrow distance between adjacent main girders, the conventional anchorage units for the external cables cannot be applied. Therefore, a novel concrete anchorage unit to be used with such bridges was developed. In this study, three-dimensional finite element analysis was conducted on a bridge strengthened by external cables using the proposed anchorage unit, to investigate the performance of the proposed anchorage unit and to quantify the effect of the strengthening. The analysis confirmed that the anchorage unit performed satisfactorily and that prestress was properly introduced to the main girders. However, it was also found that cracking occurred at the main girder near the anchorage unit. Then, it was to concluded that strengthening using the proposed anchorage unit was effective, provided that the areas prone to cracking were strengthened.

Abstract 90,000 masonry arch bridges compose the European railway system. Over time, damage appeared on structures subjected to train loads growing in number, speed and intensity. Owners of such constructions would like to understand the mechanical performance of their bridges. A global methodology including experiments and computational efficient methods is necessary to study the mechanical behavior and determine the sensitive zones of these masonry bridges. This paper presents the numerical modeling of the 'Viaduc de Saint Ouen', located near Paris (France). The numerical analysis is performed using a continuous finite element model based on an original anisotropic damage including cracks localization treatment and cracks re-closure procedure. The model uses homogenized characteristics of the masonry as data. It is implemented in the Ansys software for a three-dimensional representation. It computes the localized damage and indicates areas of preferential damage in the masonry subjected to rail loads. It is able to predict the cracking zones in large masonry constructions without pre-positioning joint elements. The reclosure function simulates the progressive stiffness restitution during the crack re-closure process. First, a concise state of the art is described and then the damage model is presented. An experimental methodology is proposed to assess homogenized data, and the damage model is finally applied to analyze the bridge. The numerical results are discussed relative to the experimental ones.

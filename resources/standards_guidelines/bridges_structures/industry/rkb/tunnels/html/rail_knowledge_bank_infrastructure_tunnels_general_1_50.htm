Title Pressure fluctuation on facilities inside tunnel under high speed train passage
 
Author Saito, H
Komura, K
Saito, S
Saito, H
Affiliation West Japan Railway Company, Osaka, Japan
Railway Technical Research Institute, Tokyo, Japan
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract In this study, focusing on “the lid of facilities having internal space such as duct and trough” as facilities inside a tunnel that may be scattered by the pressure fluctuation under high speed train passage, the possibility of lift of these facilities was evaluated quantitatively. The side ditches to drain underground water and to set up signaling cables have been set up in the vicinities of the sidewall and central passage in a tunnel. These ditches have internal space to be closed by the lid in order to prevent from a falling object and to keep a passage space for maintenance and inspection. Pressure fluctuation generated by high speed train passage generates a pressure difference between the top and reverse face of the lid. In some cases, the lid can be lifted by the pressure difference and can be scattered in the wind generated by high speed train passage. In this study, the pressure fluctuation on the top and reverse face of the lid of some facilities was measured. Based on the pressure difference, the lifting force acting on the lid was estimated. Two kinds of method for prediction of the possibility of the lid lifting were carried out. One is “comparing the maximum of lifting force with the weight of the lid” and the other is “Comparing the thickness of the lid with the estimated value of upward movement of the lid”. Furthermore, the velocity of wind generated by high speed train passage was measured above the lid. The measurement result indicates that the maximum of lifting force acts on the lid at the moment of the train tail passage. Therefore, it is considered that the pressure difference is caused because the pressure fluctuation on the reverse face cannot synchronize completely with that on the top face generated by the change of cross-section of train tail shape, and the magnitude of the pressure difference depends on the distance from the train center. Also, the wind velocity above the lid reached the maximums value, more than 50m/s immediately after the train passed through. Furthermore, with respect to the possibility of scattering, it was found that there is a possibility that the iron plate which is laid in the tunnel center passage may be lifted by the pressure fluctuation that occurs at the moment when trains pass each other, and may be scattered by the wind after a train tail passes. The results of this paper will be useful to the study of the prevention against scattering of some facilities inside a tunnel under high speed train passage.

Title Methods used in aerothermics for the Lyon-Turin transalpine tunnel project
 
Author Rety, J-M
Castel, L
Affiliation SNCF, Paris, France
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract The Italian and French railways, FS and SNCF, for the high-speed line between Lyon and Turin, plan to cross the Alps through a 50 km long tunnel. Since 1991, beginning of the feasibility study, the SNCF Research Department has been involved in the aerothermal studies of the project. Firstly, the mission consists in evaluating the impact of the aerodynamic and thermal effects on the exploitation of the tunnel. Secondly, when necessary, solutions are to be calculated in order to respect the safety and comfort criteria relative to passengers. A considerable number of parameters have to be considered. It is due to the different options remaining under examination, and the still existing inaccuracy of some physical coefficients. As a consequence, the study needs the elaboration of a numerical tool, based on engineering methods, dedicated to the aerothermics in long tunnel. The examination of the system composed by the trains and the tunnel allows to bring out four principal themes of study: air resistance to forward motion, long-term thermics, fire ventilation, pressure waves. An identical methodology is used to treat each theme. After the first calculations, using basic tools, some test measurements and three-dimensional numerical simulations were carried out. In order to conduct a parametric study, a numerical programme, called ERDAUN, was developed. Based on the previous deepened studies, this specific tool can predict the natural heating and optimise a cooling system using water pipes. An extension of the programme that includes a ventilation system is to be done in 1997 in order to study both air cooling and fire smoke propagation aspects.

Title Sound measurements as a means of condition monitoring in light railways
 
Author Lilley, DM
Affiliation University of Newcastle, Newcastle upon Tyne, UK
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract A preliminary investigation has been made of sound levels generated by passing light trains within an underground section of the Tyne and Wear Metro system which serves the city of Newcastle in North East England. Measurements were taken over a period of several hours and have shown significant variation in the maximum values of sound pressure level generated by individual trains. The equipment used for taking measurements is essentially a sound level meter and a digital voltmeter under the control of a portable computer. Special software was written to facilitate analysis of the results. Values of sound pressure level naturally increase as trains approach the site of the equipment and decrease as they continue onto their destination. The total time taken for measurement of sound generated by a single passing train varied between about 45 s and 61 s. One train which twice generated high values of sound as it passed the measurement site was later found to have missed its regular check of wheel condition and maintenance; this work had been re-scheduled to take place shortly after the day when measurements were taken. This finding provides support for the validity of the method of condition monitoring described in the paper. The effects of other factors (such as train speed) are to be investigated in the near future, when track possession and dedicated trains become available during a normal overnight closedown of the Metro system.

Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy

Abstract In the development of rail vehicles ecological considerations have been taken into account only recently. Taking the development of type H underground carriage as an example the crucial points of this begin­ning and the measures in its accomplishment are demonstrated. There are virtually three steps: Implication of a enviromental checklist defining absolute and relative requirements for rail vehicles in general. An absolute requirement represents a fundamental condition the fulfillment of which is indispens­able for the acceptance of a component, a module or a service. In contrast, by a relative require­ment a less stringent demand is expressed, which may be of more illustrative character and which in a particular and well substanciated case may be abandoned; Definition of envisaged modules and components relevant under ecological aspects, based on inve­stigations carried aut by the operating company, the 'Berliner Verkehrsbetriebe'; Discussion and explanation of the problem in total, aiming at the demonstration of conflicting tar­gets of contradictory requirements. Here it is elucidated that contradictory requirements do not only stem from the fields of noise pro­tection, maintenance, construction and production but as well from those of saftey, protection of environment and attractivness to the passenger. In this context it is pointed out that there exist technical interrelations between different targets of deve­lopment which may become apparent only in a later state of the development of the vehicle when cru­cial cornerstones of the project are fixed. Herefrom it is concluded: In order to make the inclusion of ecological aspects successful it is decisive to inspect different concepts of development in such detail so that the most important technical interrelations become recognizable. The difficulty to ralize the technical Interrelations in the early state of development already is demon­strated with the design of the passenger compartment as an example. As this has to be constructed from three-dimensional, preformed components there is no technical solution to this problem meeting all requirements at the moment. In the discussion the conflicts in development of components and moduls adjusted to ecological neces­sities are pointed out Moreover the effects of fundamental aspects of development, e.g. the operation of 6-module train that can not be split up are treated in detail.

Title Experimental analysis and numerical simulation of acoustic behaviour of a railway tunnel
 
Author Coni, M
Pau, M
Annunziata, F
Affiliation University of Cagliari, Cagliari, Italy
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract The use of numerical simulation techniques for the analysis of engineering problems allows, in many situations, to verify the effects of the application of a number of different design scenarios in a very short time, with reliable results, and at a very low cost. In case of complex structures, like railway structures, the possibility to study several relevant parameters (e.g. acoustic field) just modifying the design solutions (employed materials, geometrical characteristics, types of rolling stocks, etc..) makes it easier the designer's task, so that he can choose suitably by knowing a priori the effects which could be caused by them. The aim of this study is to build and validate, by means of experimental analysis in situ, a finite element numerical model (FEM) which reproduces the internal acoustic field of a railway tunnel, both in presence and absence of railway vehicles. The bidimensional finite element model, which represents the medial section of a tunnel of Sardinian Railways, was carried out by means of the commercial code ANSYS, using finite elements able to simulate the acoustic behaviour of the structural components of the cavity (walls, rolling stock, railway vehicle located inside the tunnel) and of the fluid that fills it (air); after numerical analysis, a number of experimental measures were performed inside the railway tunnel selected for the FEM modeling. These researches were aimed at verifying previously developed simulations, particularly regarding the recognition of the modal shapes peculiar to the cavity. For this purpose, a number of acoustic spectra was collected by stimulating the cavity with impulsive events; in these spectra resonance peaks were immediately recognized for each proper frequency. Modal shapes were taken by analyzing the signal phase between two points where two microphones were set. The harmonic analysis developed by the FEM model was then compared with the experimental results. The comparison of the results obtained in the two cases (experimental and numeric) allowed the validation of the model; in this way it was possible to exploit the advantages of numerical model properties (particularly their forecasting capabilities), in order to build a number of parallel simulations in which design variants were tested. Our results showed a good concordance between the built model and the experimental verifications, and the subsequent simulations of different design scenarios confirmed the validity and advantages of using these type of models. Therefore we have a numerical FEM model through which it is possible to establish the acoustic behaviour of a railway tunnel, starting from any sound event located in any point inside the tunnel.

Abstract This paper concerned a new aerodynamic environmental problem called 'low- frequency oscillation' relating to high speed trains. The problem emerged after the inauguration of the 270 kmfh operation by using the Shinkansen train, 'Series 300', on Sanyo-Shinkansen Line. This paper deals with three kinds of low-frequency oscillations generated by high speed trains: micro-pressure wave caused by a train nose entering the tunnel; small amplitude pressure waves radiated from a tunnel exit; and pressure fluctuation in open sections. Title Numerical simulation of compression wave propagation in German slab-track tunnels
 
Author Mitachi, T
Hieke, M

Fukuda, T
Iida, M
Saito, S
Nakamura, S
Affiliation Railway Technical Research Institute, Tokyo, Japan
DB Systemtechnik GmbH, Munich, Germany
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract A train entering a tunnel causes a micro-pressure wave (MPW) that radiates outward from the tunnel. The peak value of the MPW is approximately proportional to the maximum value of the pressure gradient of the compression wave in the tunnel. Prediction of the MPW requires evaluation of the steepening the compression wave during its propagation through a long tunnel. This paper describes the application of two techniques developed for modeling wave steepening in Japanese tunnels to 60-m2 German twin-tube tunnels: a method that uses empirical formulas for the steepening and a numerical simulation technique. First, empirical formulas are proposed for the wave steepening that occurs in the German tunnels, which have almost the same cross-sectional area as 60-m2 Japanese tunnels but different shapes. Second, experimental parameter values are proposed for the numerical simulation of 60-m2 German tunnels, and the simulation results obtained are shown to agree well with the results of field tests. Consequently, the techniques originally developed for modeling wave steepening in Japanese tunnels are concluded to be applicable to German tunnels as well.

These features make them extremely attracting for structural, environmental and in general industrial applications. In this work, optical fiber distributed temperature sensors based on spontaneous Raman scattering effect will be described, considering in particular specific applications in the sector of railway safety.

Title Railway risk sensitivity analysis: application methods
 
Author Guerrucci, L
Genovesi, P
Affiliation Rete Ferroviaria Italiana S.p.A, Rome, Italy
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract In the last decades a great interest has been observed around the opportunity, then over time became legal obligation, to make an effective risk management in all areas of human activity where this concept is applicable. In all discussions it has always been thought to reach somehow a decisive risk management in order to permanently eliminate the feeling of uncertainty and vagueness with which who is appointed to make decisions coexists. Some of the main issues are represented by identifying the effectiveness level of a specific mitigation and the priority measures to be implemented by establishing a classification according to a descending order of the estimated risk in order to optimize the adopted safety strategy. This kind of risk analysis are mainly developed throughout quantitative methods that are able to identify, by available information from databases of occurred events, the features of the systems under study and the adopted synthetic models, the parameters whose variations within confidence limits, provide a significant variation in the overall risk level. This approach derives from one of the risk analysis key criteria that considers unnecessary spending intellectual resources and unnecessary working data collection in deepening of a phenomenon if it is excluded that the depper detail does not produce a significant difference in the overall level of safety. For the purposes of the sensitivity analysis it is essential to develop the ability to exercise the significant variables through the computerization of calculation. This risk analysis application shows that it is possible to identify the effect of introducing a mitigation measure or determine a priority classification using the results of risk level calculation for different system configurations. In the discussion some operational developments concerning that framework will be reported with regard to the risk assessment in the Italian tunnels and the evaluation of effectiveness deriving from introduction of mitigation systems to the fire risk.

Title New anti-inundation measures for subway tunnels
 
Author Imamura, T
Sasai, K
Kawakami, K
Abe, T
Konishi, S
Affiliation Tokyo Metro Co,Tokyo, Japan
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract Tokyo Metro is undertaking various flood control measures to ensure the safety of passengers. These measures fall into two major categories: structural measures in which steps are taken to physically prevent water from entering underground spaces, and organizational measures taken so that passengers can evacuate safely. This report discusses measures taken based on projections of flood damage in new lines planned for construction, and new measures based on major flood damage projections recently presented by the Central Disaster Management Council and flooding projected by municipalities within the Tokyo Metropolis. The report also presents future challenges and approaches regarding flood damage to subways. New flood damage projections predict larger, farther-reaching floods than those of the past. Tokyo Metro faces many challenges in its efforts to control flooding of this scale, but we will continue to make progress as quickly as possible so that we can provide our passengers with safe, secure subway service.

Title Development of laser based tunnel scanner mounted on the guide bed
 
Subtitle Towards a global railway: proceedings
Author Sagong, M
Lee, JS
Moon, CY
Yoon, J-s
Affiliation KRRI, Uiwang, Korea
Jinion, Deajeon, Korea
Inha University, Incheon, Korea
Conference 8th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 18-22 2008
Publication Date 2008
Abstract Most railway structures require routine inspection and maintenance to extend the life cycle of the structures. In most cases routine inspection plays a major role in the determination of maintenance time and method. Structures like tunnels undergo exterior and interior inspections regularly. The typical exterior inspection of tunnel liner is mainly conducted by people. Since subjective and labor intensive processes continue for inspections, a more robust and automated inspection system is necessary. This study aims development of an automated tunnel inspection system based upon a 3 dimensional laser scanner. At the initial stage of the project, a trial model of the tunnel scanner has been developed. The performance and applicability of the system has been evaluated from the scanning of railway and subway tunnels in operation. Even though the extent of scanning is quite short, the scanned results show that the model can be applicable for different shapes and sizes of tunnels in use. The applicable size of tunnels will be limited by the range of laser sensor.

Title A study on the causes of troubles in shield tunnelling site with numerical analysis
 
Subtitle Towards a global railway: proceedings
Author Rho, BK
Choo, SY
Song, MK
Affiliation Korea Rail Network Authority, Daejeon, Korea
Danwoo E&C Co, Ltd, Sungnam, Korea
Conference 8th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 18-22 2008
Publication Date 2008
Abstract The Shield TBM tunneling method, along with other mechanized methods, is one of the preferred ways to make tunnels, especially long tunnels with unfavorable ground conditions. The Shield method is well known to be effective and safe to make tunnels in shallow and poor ground conditions. However, occasional troubles or accidents occur for mechanical or geological reasons, such as improper selection of equipment, malfunction, encounters with unexpected geological layers and large infiltrations of ground water. The Shield tunneling method constitutes open-end and closed-end types. The open-end type shield is used when the geological condition is fairly good and has enough stand-up time for at least one cycle. The closed-end type shield is used when the geological condition is so unfavorable that the tunnel face becomes unstable without instant application of support pressure. The closed-end type shield constitutes an earth-pressure-balanced shield and slurry shield. Balance between acting earth pressure and resisting support pressure is important for the closed-end type. To prevent undesirable events, not only maintenance of equipment and detailed geological investigation but also application of proper support pressure on the tunnel face is required. Both analytical solutions and numerical simulations are combined to predict proper support pressure. An analytical solution, developed by Anagnostou and Kovari(1996), is used for calculation of support pressure of EPB shield tunnel. For the numerical solution, finite difference method (FLAC) and distinctive element method (PFC) is used. The former is used for the ground reaction behavior of rock mass; the latter is used for large displacement behavior of the ground when the ground support varies. With a simple model test case for water saturated ground, the analytic solution predicts that pressures 82 kPa and 66 kPa are necessary for non-cohesive and cohesive ground conditions. For unsaturated ground, the analytic solution predicts that pressures 46 kPa and 32 kPa are necessary for non-cohesive and cohesive ground conditions. The results show consistency between analytic and numerical approaches, as the latter show a significant decline of displacement up to the predicted support pressure and minor decline is minute for more support pressure. With this study, a systematic estimation methods of tunnel face stability using a numerical and analytical solution is proposed and verified. This approach is expected to be easily adapted and to enhance the reliability of design and construction phase. Once the earth pressure is known, appropriate support pressure or stiffness can be applied for the excavation of shield tunnel.

Publication Date 2016
Abstract Research and development of advanced technologies, which are able to provide and improve the safety of rail operation, play a fundamental role in the railway sector. In this context, projects aiming at fire-detection in railway tunnels, based on optical fiber sensor systems, have been started up. These sensors provide remarkable advantages with respect to conventional electrical sensors: they do not make use of electrical or radio frequency signals and, therefore, they are immune to electromagnetic interference, their dimensions and weight are extremely small, and their operation is considered safe and reliable even in hazardous environment (e.g. in presence of high voltage, high temperature or ionizing radiations). These features make them extremely attracting for structural, environmental and in general industrial applications. In this work, optical fiber distributed temperature sensors based on spontaneous Raman scattering effect will be described, considering in particular specific applications in the sector of railway safety.

Title Way-finding lighting systems for rail tunnel evacuation: a virtual reality experiment with Oculus RiftÂ®
 
Author Cosma, G
Ronchi, E
Nilsson, D
Affiliation Department of Fire Safety Engineering, Lund University, Lund, Sweden
Source ATRI
Publication Date 2016-06
Abstract An experimental study has been conducted to investigate if a new and simple system, that is, stripes of high-bright dynamic green LED lights installed on the floor, can support people way-finding in smoke-filled railway tunnel evacuations. The impact of way-finding installations on people evacuation (intended as people movement paths and evacuation times) has been studied using Oculus Rift, a head-mounted display (HMD) device for virtual reality. Two different way-finding installation setups have been investigated. A rail tunnel evacuation scenario has been developed in a virtual environment and the behavior of 60 test participants has been observed (one control group with no installations available and two groups with either alternate or continuous high-bright dynamic green lights). Results show a positive impact of way-finding lighting installations on people evacuation safety. No significant differences have been found between the impact of dynamic alternate and continuous lights on participants' evacuation behavior. Both installation setups influenced movement paths of test participants during the evacuation process, that is, participants tend to walk closer to the lights.

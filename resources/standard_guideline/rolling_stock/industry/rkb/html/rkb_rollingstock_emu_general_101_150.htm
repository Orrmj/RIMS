Report or Manual No CRE R 22 ELS/2
Publisher Central Queensland University
Place Rockhampton, Qld, Australia
Publication Date 1995
Physical Description 17p. ; PDF
Abstract This report presents the brush related contact properties in direct current (dc) machines. It provides a brief introduction on carbon brush contact characteristics and brush operation in dc machines. Some common difficulties for sliding contacts, such as sparking, excessive heating or excessive brush wear, are also discussed. It is part of the technical information collected for further investigation on commutation problems in dc traction drives of Queensland Rail.

Title DC locomotives commutator and brush problems investigation
 
Author Zhang, J
Mathew, R
Flinders, F
Oghanna, W
Report or Manual No CRE R 21 ELS/1
Publisher Central Queensland University
Place Rockhampton, Qld, Australia
Publication Date 1995
Physical Description 17p
Abstract DC traction investigation is a trouble-shooting project co-sponsored by Queensland Rail (QR) and Central Queensland University (CQU). The aim of this project is to investigate the strategies for reducing excessive brush and commutator wears in Queensland Rail. This report gives a summary of the proposed research work on dc traction systems being conducted by Centre for Railway Engineering. Because of the complex causes for this problem, the proposed field test involves the concerns on mechanical, electrical and environmental aspects. The structure of a user friendly simulator for a dc traction motor is presented, and every sub-system in this simulator is introduced. The commutation process of traction motors, including the brush current distribution, can be visualised by means of this simulator. Another simulator introduced in the report is for brush dynamics, which will be combined with the optimum design software for designing brush rubber damper and selecting spring pressure to improve brush life.

Title Simulator of DC traction motors
 
Author Zhang, J
Mathew, R
Flinders, F
Oghanna, W
Report or Manual No CRE R 25 ELS/3
Publisher Central Queensland University
Place Rockhampton, Qld, Australia
Publication Date 1995
Physical Description 27p. ; PDF
Abstract This report presents a mathematical model and its Simulink implementation for dc traction motors. The saturation is considered via two look-up tables, which are the measured saturation curves for main poles and inter-poles respectively. Besides the saturation caused by field currents, an algorithm is derived to account for the flux weakening caused by armature reaction. The effects of saturation on all inductance parameters are presented, and variations of the armature inductance with both main pole saturation and interpole saturation are modelled. A Simulink model is developed to implement the above strategies in simulation, and can he saved in the library for further applications. By means of MATLAB software, this Simulink model can be used as a visualisation simulator for dc traction motors. The calculated waveforms of armature voltage and current under converter supply have been confirmed by measurement. This report mainly concerns the theoretical aspects of simulation on dc traction motors. The operational procedure of this simulator, including the methodology to input motor parameters, will be presented in "The users manual of Simulators for c/c traction motors".

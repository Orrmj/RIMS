Title Application of precipitation hardened copper alloy to contact wire

Kato, K
Affiliation Railway Technical Research Institute, Tokyo, Japan
Tohoku University, Sendai, Japan
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract For the speedup of train operation in electric railways, it is necessary to use a high strength contact wire with high tension in order to keep good contact with pantograph at high speed. For this purpose, Cu-Cr-Zr alloy is the most promising candidate material for contact wire because of its high strength and high conductivity. Tensile strength 550MPa, conductivity 78% IACS and good wear characteristics of 4 mm diameter wire were confirmed with basic tests. After the tests, 110 mm2 size contact wire was made, and placed for field test in commercial line. The results show that the new type contact wire has good wear characteristics and it can be used practically.

Title Data based models and machine learning classifiers applied to fault detection and diagnosis of Diesel-electric locomotives
 
Author Carmo, DA
Munaro, CJ
Affiliation Vale S.A., Vitória-ES, Brazil
Federal University of Espírito Santo, Vitória-ES, Brazil
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th International Heavy Haul Conference, (IHHA 2019), Achieving breakthrough performance levels, 12-14 June, 2019, Narvik, Norway
Publication Date 2019
Abstract Diesel-electric locomotives play an important role in railway composition traction. Failures affect operational efficiency negatively and the time required to predict or isolate a fault is the most important factor to mitigate them. Many sensors are available in diesel engine for control and protection. However, alarms are usually provided when a shutdown is required to avoid engine damage. In this work a diagnosis system is developed to anticipate failures in the diesel engine cooling systems. Data from sensors is used to fit data-based models to generate residuals that are used for fault detection. Features of these residuals are used to train three classifiers for fault diagnosis: artificial neural network, decision trees and k-nearest neighbors. Changes in the rotation speed are considered since all signals are affected and may produce false alarms. Different data sets were used for training, validation and test, using data produced with the diesel engine under normal and faulty operation. Two faults were considered: reduction in the pressure of the cooling water and leakage. The tests showed that both faults were correctly diagnosed in less than 10s, which is a very good result since the engine could operate for more than 1h under these faulty conditions.

Title Research for contact loss reduction method by damping catenary vibration
 
Author Aboshi, M
Manabe, K
Affiliation Railway Technical Research Institute, Tokyo, Japan
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract For high-speed operation in electric railway, reducing the contact loss of pantograph is one of the important subjects. This paper is concerned with the vibration of thecontact wire and the pantograph in high-speed running. In order to investigate into the relations between the contact force fluctuation of the pantograph and the wave motion of the contact wire, and to work out an effective method, theoretical analyses, experiments and field tests have been carried out. From the results thereof, at a frequency where the contact wire is regarded as a string, it is confirmed that the magnitude of the contact force fluctuation is in proportion to the vertical velocity of the wave motion incident to the pantograph. And the measured values in these experiments agree well with the theoretical results. When the wave of the contact wire is reflected completely at the support point in single wire equipment, the wave motion runs repeatedly between the support point and the pantograph, and the resulting contact force fluctuation becomes comparatively large. Therefore, at each hanger point in the catenary system, it is desirable that the energy consumption of wave motion be large and the reflection factor be small. A hanger developed to have spring and damping mechanism, such as a rubber damping hanger or a friction damping hanger, is found to be effective for damping the catenary vibration and reducing the contact loss. It is expected that the contact loss reduction method by damping the catenary vibration will contribute to speed-up in electric railway.

Title Fast and precise position measurement of contact wires by a laser scanning system
 
Author Höfler, H
Dimopoulos, N
Metzger, B
Egiseer, F
Affiliation Fraunhofer-Institute of Physical Measurement Techniques (IPM), Freiburg, Germany
Plasser & Theurer, Linz, Austria
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract A system for contactiess and fast measuring height and stagger of the contact wire is presented. The sensor unit of the system is mounted on the roof of the measuring car. It includes a laser, optics, and electronics for scanning the laser beam over an angle of 600 towards the catenary. By modulating the laser intensity and using a phase measurement technique the distance to any object hit by the laser beam is gauged. A robust and air conditioned housing protects the sensor against enviromental impacts. It is connected to the power unit and the data evaluation unit respectively, both placed inside the car. The transmission of the distance values and scanner position data is carried out by an optical fiber to avoid any electromagnetic interferences. The data evaluation unit comprises a standard PC upgraded with a powerfull digital signal processor board. Software tools for identifying up to 4 wires within the angle of view, calculating their height and stagger position in relation to the car body and transferring the results in anlog or digital recording systems are available. If additional data of the lateral tilt of the car body are accessible, the position is calculated in relation to the track. Measurement frequency is 100 Hz, the overall accuracy for height and stagger measurement is ± 5 mm.

Abstract Electrical railway networks are large and complex dynamic systems. In the analysis of such systems, frequency-domain methods like the Fourier transform are a standard tool, as in many other fields of science and engineering. However, in many practical situations neither a pure time-domain analysis nor a pure frequency-domain analysis is particularly suitable for revealing the dynamics of a system under investigation. This is especially true for the analysis of transient effects in electrical railway networks, where a large number of vehicles and substations are directly connected. While engineers have long been taught not to mix the time and the frequency domain, the present contribution applies the concept of time-frequency analysis to railway networks, using basis functions that have both a defined time of their occurrence and a defined frequency. These so-called wavelets often represent a good compromise between the two worlds. After an introduction to wavelets and a brief survey of available analysis tools, several practical data sets related to electrical system compatibility in rail networks are examined, and wavelet analysis is found to be a valuable new analysis tool, particularly for transient effects. Wavelets have two main advantages. First, the concept of a time-frequency plane is very beneficial in the analysis of transients, and can be visualized in an intuitive way. Second, wavelets avoid to "average out" transient harmonics, as Fourier analysis often does, and even permit to find cause-effect relations in the data by analyzing frequency content while preserving the original timing.

Abstract Stimulated by the scarcity and the increasing price of energy, the European Union and the top-level French meeting on the environment (le Grenelle de l'Environnement) tend to make the most important companies aware of energy saving and trading. Receptive to this demand, and even if rail transport is considered as a clean transport mode, the SNCF group (French National Railway Company) proposes new sustainable development approaches in order to improve its energy performance, in particular through the Department of the Electric Traction of the Direction of the Engineering (IG-TE) which, within the context of its designer's role of installation of electric traction, is a driving element in the development of innovations. Even though the use of "clean" energy electricity combined to the latest years' technical evolutions, enable transportation systems to develop more efficiently and ecologically. SNCF is still a big energy consumer and electrical traction represents 90% of the whole SNCF energy consumption. This energy consumption must be optimised for a more energy efficient railway. That is why the SNCF Engineering Department and SNCF Research Department are looking at every option: the study for a better use of braking energy is one of the solutions. To work, the electric rolling stock needs a power supply network corresponding to their drive chain. Linked with the High-voltage network, the electric traction substations convert the energy with transformer-rectifier groups to supply with the 1500V in direct current. The energy forwarded by catenary is consumed by motors during the driving phases. Conversely, during the braking phases, the traction motors can send back into the catenary the energy produced during these braking phases. The installations on the 25 000V alternative power supply network allow naturally the recovery of the braking energy. On the RFF's current 1500V power supply network, this recovered energy can be consumed only by the train's cruisers in the driving phase. However there are applications of small powers under 750V in direct current (Subway or Tramway types for instance) which allow the storage of this energy by ultracapacitors or flywheels. For installations with heavy powers under 1500V (typical Regional Express Train, Intercity), which use the unidirectional structure of the converters used in substation, the absence of systems of recovery or trains cruisers implies that electrical energy cannot go back through the power supply network during the braking phases of trains. The advantages of the electric traction for the exploitation of the railway lines with high traffic are widely recognized. To continue to develop this transport by improving energy efficiency, modern rolling stock is equipped with a system which allows the return of electrical energy during braking. It represents a possibility to avoid the wasting of already produced energy and so, to decrease electrical consumption. The introduction of a new system named 'Active Substation' which uses new generation components not only allows the recovery of energy, but also complementary functions.

Title Optimal strategy driving for traction energy saving

Abstract SNCF is one of the first energy consumers in France. Its energy bill represents nearly one billion Euros, out of which 82% is electrical energy for traction. Moreover, the current environmental context aims at reducing carbon emissions and general power consumption. Thus, improving traction energy efficiency is an essential research subject for SNCF, both economically and environmentally. Several axis may be followed to achieve this goal: improvement of the material aerodynamic characteristics, improvement on the power supply system, or improvement on the driving strategy. In this paper the focus is on the third axis, the driving strategy. The aim is to define the optimal driving strategy in terms of energy consumption; this must not be achieved without taking into account the constraints induced by the commercial timetable. The optimization presented here provides an optimal train path subject to these time constraints. It is based on a dichotomy search method. This paper introduces the problem in more detail, then give the results from numerical experiments and concludes on the efficiency of the algorithm presented and on further works.

Abstract This paper introduces technical characteristics, installation operation data and expected economical effect about alternating current (AC) energy storage system (ESS) at the high speed operation commercial line in Korea. Compared to DC type ESS, the regenerative power recycling rate is lower because of the distance between substations and train operation diagram. Also additional equipment is necessary, for instance, inverter/converter, transformer, Chopper and etc. Various examinations must be preceded before applying the system. In the case of the Kyung-Bu high speed line, huge train load (KTX, 13,560kw) moves along the track which causes a negative effect to the power quality, including voltage drop and variation. These effects give fatigue and outage to the electrical component at the train. This point of view main focus of the AC ESS must satisfy the voltage regulation stabilization, reducing peak power consumption and power factor improvement including saving energy. Typical system design including operation scheme is introduced, which is proper to the local site. To improve the system performance, different type of storage device is connected and controlled each other. Simulation has been made to verify the effect of the system and finally onsite operation data is collected.

Title Traction power system capacity limitations at various traffic levels
 
Subtitle Meeting the challenges for future mobility: proceedings
Author Abrahamsson, L
Söder, L
Affiliation Royal Institute of Technology (KTH), Stockholm, Sweden
Conference 9th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 22-26 2011, Lille, France
Publication Date 2011
Abstract The aim, and main contribution, of this paper is to propose a fine-tuned fast approximator, based on neural networks, that uses aggregated traction system information as inputs and outputs. This approximator can be used as an investment planning constraint in the optimization. It considers that there is a limit on the intensity of the train traffic, depending on the strength of the power system. In the numerical examples of this paper, the approximator inputs are the power system configuration, the distance between a connection from contact line to the public grid to another connection, and the average number of trains for that distance. The output is the maximal attainable average velocity of trains of a specific kind for the by the inputs described railway power system section. An alternative output - the traveling time is also presented. The main emphasis of this paper is on the example section, since the contribution of this paper is mainly to show on the improved simplicity and reality compliance. The applicative contribution is twofold, an improved TPSA as a planning/decision making program constraint, whereas it also can be used as a scientifically developed rule of thumb for a planner active in the field. The aim is not primarily to show that the idea works, or to motivate the principal idea, since that is done earlier. The approximator facilitates studies of many railway power system loading scenarios, combined with different power system configurations, for investment planning analysis. The approximator is based on neural networks. An additional value of the approximator is that it provides an understanding of the relations between power system configuration and train traffic performance.

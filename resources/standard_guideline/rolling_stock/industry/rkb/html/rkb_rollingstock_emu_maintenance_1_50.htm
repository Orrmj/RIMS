Title Contact wire wear measurement devices. A system comparison
 
Author Muller, R
Affiliation IPM Fraunhofer-Institut for Physical Measurement Techniques, Freiburg, Germany
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract The contact line, beside the rails, is one of the most important part of electrified railway systems. With increasing speed, economic awareness and a closer focus on quality and performance the awareness of a problem is growing: the wear of the contact wire. On many tracks the wear measurement is still accomplished by hand and only at specific locations, always obstructing the traffic. Presently, different system approaches are appearing worldwide, which offer the possibility to measure the wear at train speed. The wear of the contact wire can be deduced from the measurement of one of six different contact wire properties. Either electrical or optical measurement methods are proposed. General aspects of the wear measurement of contact wires are presented and 13 well-established measuring systems and recently developed systems are cited and compared with the emphasis on optical measurement systems.

Title The upgrading of both electrical and eletromechanical obsolete components reduces maintenance costs and improves the performance of the rolling stock
 
Author Fusarpoli, P
Mocerino, B
Baione, U
Affiliation Ferrovie dello Stato S.p.A., Firenze, Italy
Ansaldo Trasporti S.p.A., Napoli, Italy
Angiolo Siliani S.p.A., Firenze, Italy
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract The aim of railways companies and constructors is to realize tractive stock which present a high degree of compliance with reliability, availability and maintainability parameters (RAM articles) and which can preserve them in time. In order to get such results it is necessary for the designing of the tractive stock, with specific reference to electric locomotives, to keep in due account general concepts as redundancy, testability, maintainability, by employing high reliability components. Besides the necessary attention paid to these parameters, today greater importance is assigned to environmental conditions of the inside of the electric locomotives, on one side with the purpose of improving the comfort of engineers and on the other side to guarantee an increasing running evenness, by providing a driving system which, in case of breakdown, indicates the most suitable actions to be taken to resume a correct running. All the above mentioned aims for newly designed electric locomotives, meant to improve general running conditions, can be today successfully applied to more than fifteen year old electric locomotives, for which the problem of the obsolescence of materials rises, in the first place regarding the electronic components, by an upgrading of electronic units and electromechanical components. Further emphasis on such problem is put by the lack of components on the market and the disappearance of secondary sources. The obsolescence of materials can on one hand worsen the reliability and on the other hand increase maintenance costs, by the rise both of diagnosis and repair times, and make it sometime necessary to have recourse to circuit adaptations. A further purpose of this paper is to stress how through the upgrading redundancy can be introduced and testability can be improved, reaching the primary goal of reducing maintenance costs and improving running evenness. Some examples of upgrading of electronic, mechanical and electromechanical devices will be described, referring to E6321E633 locomotives. Furthermore a concrete example of the application of such a method will be illustrated, referring to the second series of E656 electric locomotives.

Title Deterioration and performance analyses of subway electric multiple units
 
Subtitle Towards a global railway: proceedings
Author Kim, J
Seo, JW
Kwon, ST
Affiliation Korea Railroad Research Institute, Uiwang, South Korea
Conference 8th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 18-22 2008
Publication Date 2008
Abstract In order to guarantee the performance and safety during the operation of subway electric multiple units (EMUs), which were used for over 20 years, it is required to assess the current deterioration and reliability conditions for EMU components as well as the whole systems. Also, according to the Korean urban transit railway law, in order to validate the extension possibility for the legal lifetime of 25 years in subway EMUs, the current condition assessment, safety assessment, remaining lifetime evaluation, etc. need to be performed. Based on the precision diagnosis results, the legal lifetime of subway rolling stocks could be extended up to 5 years. In this investigation, the EMUs used for over twenty years were selected for deterioration and reliability evaluation. The subway rolling stocks used for this investigation were composed of 6 EMUs including 2 control cars, 2 motor cars, and 2 trailer cars; they were the chopper controlled subway rolling stocks. Various types of engineering analysis techniques were used for the current performance and safety evaluation. Several characterization means, including nondestructive evaluation (NDE) techniques, corrosion testing, and three-dimensional measurements, were employed for the evaluation of car bodies and bogies in EMUs. The NDE techniques include ultrasonic testing for the measurement of thickness changes and flaw detection, magnetic particle testing for the detection of surface flaws, and radiography method for the defects in weldments. In order to confirm the changes in dimension of car body or bogies, three dimensional measurements were performed using the laser tracker. The measurement results were compared with the dimensions in original drawing. Furthermore, stress and structural analyses using commercial finite element method (FEM) software provided important information on stress distribution and load transfer mechanisms, and the FEM results were compared with running safety testing results during test running. After deterioration analyses of EMUs, in order to investigate performance characteristics of deteriorated subway rolling stocks, several engineering assessment methods, such as the acceleration, vibration, and braking performance tests, were carried out on subway EMUs.

Title An integrated maintenance information system for EMU
 
Subtitle Towards a global railway: proceedings
Author An, T-K
Park, K-J
Chung, J-D
Kim, M-H
Affiliation Korea Railroad Research Institute, Uiwang, Korea Sungkunkwan University, Suwon, Korea
Conference 8th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 18-22 2008
Publication Date 2008
Abstract Urban transit operation corporations perform repetitive works to investigate the electric multiple units, by fixed maintenance cycle. The quality of maintenance work usually depends on workers' 'know-how'. Though some information to support maintenance work is digitized and provided to workers, it is not of great help to them because of its non-systematic structure. Information includes drawings, manuals, work results, work history, fault history, etc. Maintenance workers want to access the information, anytime anywhere, and make an effort to promote the efficiency of maintenance work for EMU. However, it is difficult to achieve that without some structured information system which is able to be used in the works. So a maintenance information system for EMU has been developed for about 4 years, from 2002 to 2005. In this paper the configurations and characteristics of the system, and the results of the certification test are presented.

Title Assessment of electrical commutation characteristics of DC traction motors by measurement of brush wafer currents
 
Author Flinders, F
Zhang, J
James, K
Oghanna, W
Affiliation Queensland Rail (QR)
KCRC
EDI Rail
ARRI
Source Railway Technical Society of Australasia (RTSA)
Conference 7th International Heavy Haul Conference, Confronting the barriers of heavy haul rail technology, 10-14 June 2001, Brisbane, Queensland, Australia
Publication Date 2001
Abstract The commutation of characteristics of the DC traction motor is of critical importance to minimising maintenance costs. A badly commutating machine will cause the commutator to go out of round very quickly and the brush wear rate will subsequently rise to an unacceptable level. The traditional method of determining the commutation condition is to conduct a blackband test. However this is not normally done with a DC supply in a static situation. This does not represent real operating conditions and does not replicate the various factors which tend to disrupt good commutation. This paper presents a method of assessing the commutation characteristics of the traction motor in normal service conditions. This paper is capable of revealing the influence of AC ripple in the DC armature and field supplies.

Title High voltage AC system topologies for the supply of DC traction railways
 
Author Stuart-Smith, D
Affiliation Arup, NSW, Australia
Source Railway Technical Society of Australasia (RTSA)
Conference CORE 2014, Rail Transport for a Vital Economy, Conference on Railway Excellence, Adelaide, South Australia, 5-7 May, 2014
Publication Date 2014-05
Abstract For an electric railway the loss of traction power supply can instantaneously paralyse part or all of the rail system with the impacts being felt a long way from the site of the incident that caused the power failure. In addition to operational disruption, prolonged loss of traction supply can give rise to adverse consequences such as passengers being stranded in trains without functioning air-conditioning which can be quite serious on hot days. This paper discusses the advantages and disadvantages of various high voltage network connection topologies and busbar configurations for the supply of railway DC traction systems. The outcomes for the traction system are a function of both the configuration of the rail system, and the characteristics of the electricity host network. A sub-transmission network dedicated to the railway and which connects to multiple supply points will usually improve security of supply. However, ultimately the upstream networks will be interconnected and so are vulnerable to common-mode failures which must be considered. While operating such a network can provide a great improvement in security of supply and flexibility for maintenance, there is also significant investment required and the whole-of-life integrated logistics support implications of having an in-house network are significant. These issues are explored in the first part of this paper along with appropriate incident response and back-up strategies. The second part of the paper covers possible busbar configuration options appropriate to different voltage levels and switchgear technologies. Discussion includes the impact of the maintenance requirements of different technologies on the number of isolators and circuit breakers required. Examples are drawn from the author’s practical experience in Sydney, Melbourne and Kuala Lumpur, and the paper discusses the features of examples that are relevant to benchmarking future proposals. The paper demonstrates that different configurations are appropriate to different circumstances and gives practical examples.

Title Intelligent comprehensive inspection system/solution for EMUs first-level maintenance on entrance line
 
Author Zhang, Y
Peng, J
Yang, K
Zhao, B
Peng, C
Gao, X
Wang, Z
Ma, L
Affiliation School of Physics Science and Technology, Southwest Jiaotong University, Chengdu
Chengdu Lead Science & Technology Co., Ltd., Chengdu, China
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), railway research to enhance the customer experience, 28 October-1 November 2019, Tokyo, Japan
Publication Date 2019
Abstract Growing rail traffic worldwide requests higher safety standards and economical maintenance cost at the same time. A set of integrated intelligent inspection solution gradually formed for security inspection of EMUs on the entrance line after several years’ exploration and research. Level 1 maintenance includes wheel surface defects inspection, wheel inner defects inspection, pantograph slider wear inspection, brake disk wear inspection, abnormal fans inspection, the loosening and loss of bolts for key components. The paper mainly introduces the intelligent comprehensive inspection solution for EMUs first-level maintenance on the entrance line. The first part will introduce the whole process of intelligent human-computer interaction operation, from automatic detection by wayside inspection module, manual analysis and confirmation, manual maintenance record to recheck. Then this paper will introduce technical principle and technical difficulties in this solution. Pattern recognition and deep learning technology are used to recognize and locate some key parts for a safety inspection, and acoustic technology is also applied in this solution to detect fans. Moreover, 3D technology is used to reduce the interference of false alarm caused by water, dust and sunshine. The last of the paper will introduce application management and data comprehensive analysis platform which can improve and strengthen the quality and efficiency of CRH repair work.

Title Wearing models for pantograph stripes condition-based maintenance
 
Author Biancucci, A
Menci, M
Affiliation Trenitalia S.p.A. – Technical Department, Florence, Italy
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), railway research to enhance the customer experience, 28 October-1 November 2019, Tokyo, Japan
Publication Date 2019
Abstract Maintenance costs related to consumables components have always played an important role on maintenance plans. Nowadays, soft maintenance activities on regional passenger transportation locomotives like E464 fleet are based on scheduled controls, checks and visual inspections to be performed each 20000 km covered by locomotives. These actions, that are mainly based on experience matured during past years on railway field, are time and cost consuming considering the widespread actuation on all fleets and moreover don’t provides often the expected benefits. The introduction of Telediagnostic systems on board trains has provided a strong impulse to the component remote monitoring, allowing real data acquisition (coming from sensors, vehicle logic command and control data, …) for the analysis. This paradigm change has opened wide perspectives for condition-based maintenance algorithms creation, that has made possible the disjunction between distance covered by locomotive and component exploitation during this: following this way it has become possible the assessment of real component status or its percentage of life expired (compared to maximum admitted life declared by the manufacturer), enhancing the availability and knowledge levels for monitored components. A direct effect of this revolutionary and innovative framework has been the maintenance plans optimization according to these algorithms implemented. On this paper it’s showed an example of that powerful revolution applied on rolling stock owned by Trenitalia, and realized by means of the introduction of pantograph stripes wearing models based on real stress factors that the fleet has to withstand during the commercial service. In details, after a general overview about perspectives opened by Telediagnostic system, it will be shown the process of data analysis, the model implementation, the campaign of experimental measures for data validation and the algorithm implementation on the ground system. Eventually a focus on possible future improvements for the algorithm will be provided.

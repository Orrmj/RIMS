Title Wind-induced accidents of train/vehicles in Japan and the countermeasures on Kansai airport line
 
Author Fujii, T
Tanemoto, K
Maeda, T
Imai, T
Ishihama, J
Kawashima, K
Affiliation Railway Technical Research Institute, Tokyo, Japan
West Japan Railway Company, Osaka, Japan
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract There were 28 wind-induced serious accidents of train/vehicles reported since the establishment of Japanese Railways in 1872. Especially a strong wind accident of train/vehicles in 1986 happened at famous Amarube bridge which was a plate-girder 41m high above the ground. For the purpose of clarifying the causes of the accident on the bridge and of seeking measures for the safety of operation in a strong wind, a committee was set up. The committee suggested several proposals for the improvement of train stop method in a strong wind. In these circumstances, construction of Kansai International Airport was planned to operate until 1994. This airport is located on an artificially constructed island. It is connected to the Kansai district with a bridge 3750m long and 50m high above the sea level in the Osaka bay. Railways and auto roads run on the bridge. We have never experienced operating trains on such a long bridge. For planning the operation, we analyzed the data of strong winds measured near a small tower situated 15m above the sea level for the past 15 years prior to the constriction of the airport. And we also carried out wind-tunnel tests using scale models of the bridge and vehicles passing through the bridge to evaluate the aerodynamic force acting on the vehicles. In consideration of the rise in instantaneous wind velocity, the time trains take to pass through the bridge, the critical wind velocity for overturning of the vehicles, and the gust factor of winds, we determined the wind velocity to stop and resume operating of trains. As the result of these studies, Kansai airport line is operating with no accident induced by strong winds.

Title Investigation into Waterfall derailment
 
Author Donaldson, K
Affiliation Independent Transport Safety and Reliability Regulator
Source Permanent Way Institution (PWI)
Conference Permanent Way Institution (PWI) NSW 2004 Annual Convention: Rail Resurgence
Publication Date 2004-10-29
Abstract On 31 January 2003, a passenger train service from Sydney to Port Kembla overturned at high speed and collided with stanchions and a rock cutting near Waterfall NSW. The train driver and six passengers were killed. The Ministry of Transport completed its investigation into the incident in early 2004, delivering 66 safety recommendations. The Special Commission of Inquiry is due to deliver its final report later this year. This paper describes some aspects of the investigations conducted into the causes of the incident and the responses of some of the various agencies involved.

Title A new derailment evaluation method of high speed railway vehicle
 
Author Yao, J
Sun, L
Affiliation Railway Science & Technology Research & Development Center of China Academy of Railway Science, Beijing, China
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract There is a big difference of derailment under high-speed and low-speed conditions. In the case of low-speed condition, the derailment is approximated as a quasi-static climb derailment process, while in the high-speed condition, the wheel-rail contact force and the wheel moving posture relative to the rail must be considered carefully in the derailment process. Consequently, the derailment in high-speed condition should be investigated by different model and evaluated by different method compared with derailment in low-speed condition. This paper takes the wheel-rail nonlinear interaction theory as a key point, by introducing the wheel and rail actual profiles and the excitation conditions, a nonlinear dynamic model which is suitable for the analysis of dynamic performance of the high speed vehicle is established. A new derailment on-line monitor method for high speed railway vehicle is proposed, which is based on the numerical study of dynamic performance of high speed vehicle. The new criterion set a dynamic variable which is closely related to wheel-rail interaction as the online monitoring parameter, comparing with the Nadal derailment coefficient which is much more suitable for the quasi-steady wheel climb derailment condition, the new evaluation method can well explain the reason why derailment occurred especially for the high speed running condition. Numerical simulation result shows that the new method can monitor the high speed derailment and is easy to implement engineering applications.

Abstract Why?" is a very powerful question and we us it a lot. In derailment investigation it is the question underlying all that we do. The answers we get enable us to:
- establish how the derailment occurred
- who or what was responsible for the derailment and

Title Systemic factors in the investigation of railway occurrences in South Africa
 
Author Hutchings, J
Affiliation Transnet Centre of Systems Engineering, University of the Witwatersrand, Johannesburg, South Africa
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 11th International Heavy Haul Conference, (IHHA 2017), Advancing heavy haul technologies and operations in a changing world, 2-6 September, 2017, Cape Town, South Africa
Publication Date 2017
Abstract Railway occurrences in South Africa remain high, despite occurrence investigations. The primary aim of an investigation is to uncover the events at all levels of the system and to identify remedial actions to prevent a recurrence. If accidents are being investigated, why then do the number of occurrences remain largely unchanged? It is proposed that perhaps there are aspects or factors within the railway system that influences the investigation process and therefore its effectiveness. The objectives of this research are to understand what systemic factors (challenges / pressures / frustrations / obstacles) influence the investigation process of railway occurrences. A qualitative multi-method approach was adopted in this research. The results illustrate that systemic factors do influence the investigation process. Examples of systemic factors identified include: no National Rail Policy for the country, financial constraints, resource issues and inadequate training. It can be confirmed that constraints and challenges higher up in the system influence the actual process of conducting investigations, influencing the effectiveness of occurrence investigations. This research contributes to a deeper understanding of the nature of accident investigations.

Title Application of fault tree analysis and swiss cheese model to the overspeed derailment of Puyuma train in Yilan, Taiwan
 
Author Jong, J
Lai, Y
Young, C
Chen, Y-F
Affiliation Civil, Hydraulic Engineering & Railway Transportation Research Center, Sinotech Engineering Consultants, Inc., Taipei
Department of Civil Engineering, Railway Technology Research Center, National Taiwan University, Taipei
Railway Bureau, Ministry of Transportation and Communications (MOTC), New Taipei City
Source ATRI
Journal Transportation Research Record
Publication Date 2020
Abstract On October 21, 2018, a Puyuma express train went overspeed through a sharp curve and derailed in Yilan, Taiwan. This accident resulted in 18 fatalities and 267 injuries. Although such accidents occur once in a while worldwide, this case of an overspeed derailment from a train-set equipped with an automatic train protection (ATP) system (similar to the function of Positive Train Control (PTC) in the U.S.) is rare. A temporary investigation team was appointed by the Executive Yuan, the highest administrative organ in Taiwan, and the investigation was completed within 2 months. This paper presents the process, analysis, findings, and recommendations from the accident investigation. The accident was first analyzed using fault tree analysis to identify potential causes and contributing factors of this derailment. The results were then categorized into layers of defenses by using a Swiss cheese model. We further extended the original Swiss cheese model to a ‘time-dependent Swiss cheese model’ to demonstrate how the barriers were penetrated at different times by incorporating the timestamps of important events. Another modified Swiss cheese model called ‘causal relationship Swiss cheese model’ was presented to further demonstrate the causal relationships. With the proposed process and models, the immediate causes and contributing factors were quickly identified and presented in a way that could be easily understood by the general public. The results showed that the ATP system (or the PTC) cannot guarantee 100 percent safety. A review of the safety culture and corresponding procedures is important to ensure the safety of railway operations.

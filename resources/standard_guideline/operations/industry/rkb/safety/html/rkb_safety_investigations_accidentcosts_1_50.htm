Title Heavy haul freight train severity and consequence analysis in North America
 
Author Wang, B
Kim, J
Barkan, CPL
Affiliation Rail Transportation and Engineering Center – RailTEC
University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, Urbana, IL USA
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th International Heavy Haul Conference, (IHHA 2019), Achieving breakthrough performance levels, 12-14 June, 2019, Narvik, Norway
Publication Date 2019
Abstract Heavy haul freight railways play an important role supporting North American economy. Freight train derailment rates have declined considerably over the past decade; however, when they do occur they can damage infrastructure and rolling stock, interfere with transportation, and may cause causalities and releases of dangerous goods. Derailment analysis and prevention has long been a high priority for the rail industry and policy makers. Therefore, it is imperative to investigate both train derailment severity and consequences. Most previous research has focused on analysis of factors affecting the probability of train derailments, but less research has been conducted on understanding factors affecting derailment severity estimation. This paper aims to identify variables affecting derailment severity and develop a new methodology for estimation. Extensive, detailed data from the U.S. Department of Transportation Federal Railroad Administration (FRA) were used to construct and compare several machine learning models for train derailment severity prediction, including multiple regression, KNN, regularized regression (ridge and LASSO), random forest, and gradient boosting. The results can be used to provide insights for the industry to understand factors affecting derailment severity and provide suggestions to develop prediction models.

Title Preventive measures for train drivers’ drowsiness during driving
 
Author Senda, T
Fukuma, K
Hayashi, M
Affiliation West Japan Railway Company, Osaka, Japan
Hiroshima University, Higashihiroshima, Japan
Source ACRI
Conference 11th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), research and innovation from today towards 2050, 29 May-2 June 2016, Milan, Italy
Publication Date 2016
Abstract Sleepiness in train drivers when operating trains is a significant risk factor for major accidents. The possibility of accidents caused by sleepiness is also a significant social problem, which has been given much attention in recent years. In the past, our company has implemented various countermeasures against sleepiness. However, sleepiness in train drivers when operating trains has remained a crucial problem. Investigating and developing preventive interventions for sleepiness that agree with the sleep mechanisms of drivers, and their operating style are needed. In this article, we report past studies and their findings regarding the problem of sleepiness among train drivers, and current studies that are being conducted in our department.

Title Progress of safety in Japanese railways: accidents investigation, countermeasures and R & D of safety technologies
 
Author Matsumoto, A
Journal Journal of Mechanical Systems for Transportation and Logistics
Publication Date 2010-01
Abstract Nowadays the safety level of Japanese railways is very high, as 0.6 accidents happen per 106 km train-run and nearly zero on-board passengers are killed in a normal year. It is the fruits of technologies for safety and the efforts of people involved in railways. But unfortunately we had not a few serious accidents in the past. In this paper the author reports the outline of accident occurrences, countermeasures against them and safety progress in last 50 years in Japan, and explains the reorganization of the accident investigation body to newly established JTSB

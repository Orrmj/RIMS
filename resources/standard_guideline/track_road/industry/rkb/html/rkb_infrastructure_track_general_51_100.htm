Title Track/bridge interaction
 
Author Ramondenc, P
Affiliation SNCF, Paris, France
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract Track/bridge interaction is an important phenomenon; the risk of track buckling and rail fracture affects safety and the presence of expansion devices affects passenger comfort. The phenomenon of interaction forms the subject of this paper. Various track and bridge parameters will be covered, with the important factor of track behaviour being covered in some detail. A former UIC Working Group carried out an initial study on the basis of the current rules contained in ENV 1991 : 1994, Section 3.6. A number of different software packages are available, and these have been used to carry out sensitivity calculations, which are also described here. The aim of ERRl D 213's work was to produce diagrams for a simple case (simply-supported deck and continuous deck with fixed bearing at one end). The diagrams were to allow interaction effects to be determined as a function of deck span. Both track with and track without expansion devices was studied. The Committee has also drawn up rules for the calculation of more complex cases, using the above-mentioned graphs in conjunction with appropriate formulas.

Title Technical and economical track assessment using simulation models
 
Author Ripke, B
Affiliation Deutsche Bahn AG, Munich, Germany
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract The difficulties of the railways to design and assess a track construction for today's or future demands are pointed out. As a solution a knowledge based system is proposed, which integrates existing models, measurements and knowledg~ for the different aspects of the short-term and long-term behaviour of track. The concept of the knowledge based system for technical and economical track assessment is presented. In a first step the system behaviour, which means the feedback between the short-term dynamics of the vehicle and the track and the long-term behaviour of the track, will be simulated. Due to the closed loop the dependencies and sensitivities of track, vehicle and operational parameters on the short and long-term behaviour of the track can be shown. In a second step the life cycle costs will be estimated and important boundary conditions such as availability, noise or comfort will be monitored taking as input the simulated long-term behaviour. Since the influence of the parameters on the system behaviour are known from the first step, the influence of the parameters on the life cycle costs is intelligible and therefore system optimisations regarding lower life cycle costs are possible. The core of the knowledge based system is a data base management system, which stores the measured and calculated results, evaluations, methods and the particular knowledge. All programs will work in an integrated environment, which controls the input data, the run of the programs, the data flow between the programs and the evaluation of data. After a simulation the calculated results and the used methods will be stored in the database of the system to use them for further evaluations. Due to sophisticated evaluation methods and retrieval functions the stored results are connectable and available for all users of the system. The results of a preliminary study point out that the development of the knowledge based system is possible within the next years.

Author Carabelli, S
Deiprete, C

Genta, G
Muzzarelli, M
Zanolli, S
Affiliation Politecnico di Torino, Torino, Italy
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract The aim of the present paper is to describe a maglev system based on permanent magnets operating in repulsion which is at present studied within the frame of the Second Finalized Transportation Programme (PFT2) of Italian National Research Council (CNR) and to summarize the experimental results obtained on a demonstrator. The mathematical models which were used to design the control system and the demonstrator, consisting of a levitating platform with mass of 200 - 600 kg, free to move on a length of 2 m of magnetic rail, are described. The control system is based on an observer implemented on a digital computer with a DSP board which allows to perform a state control without the need of actually measuring the lateral and yaw velocities. A couple of hail probes, located at the front and rear ends of the platform, supply the lateral positions. A feedback on the states estimated is used to regulate the system. The experimental results are in good accordance with the numerical results obtained from the mathematical model of the device and demonstrate that the system works as expected.

Unfortunately, even in developed countries in the 21st century there will be some lines or line sections which will still maintain mixed traffic, i.e. traffic with different train categories, and this will be even more the case in the developing countries, often even on main trunk lines. Therefore today, but also in future, the outstanding question is how to establish capacity and choose the solutions under such conditions. This problem becomes complicated in particular when two double-track lines with mixed traffic are to be converged, and then carry out the restructuring of traffic and enable the exit onto two new double-track lines, but with desired train categories, still maintaing the satisfactory quality of transport service, and the railway's competitiveness, as well. The model also provides essential elements, such as the rational capacity regarding the quality of service, essential elements for establishing the operating costs, investment cost estimate and the application of"Cost-benefit" method, as well as the methods of multiple-criterion ranking for choice of solutions for modernization of such lines.

Title An evaluation methodology for innovative mass transit system projects that combines performance and landscape and environmental items
 
Author Pratelli, A
Costa, S
Affiliation University of Pisa, Pisa, Italy
University of Florence, Firenze, Italy
Conference World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR): optimizing costs and productivity while respecting the environment, 16-19 November 1997, Florence, Italy
Publication Date 1997
Abstract This paper deals with some aspects of the strategic planning of a non conventional, or innovative, mass transit system located in a heavy urbanized context. A two stages design methodology is developed to assess both the innovative system type to adopt and its own network layout related to the study context. This proposed methodology is based on multicriteria analysis procedures for project optimization and decision support. The first stage involves the choice of the type of innovative system that better fits to a given context and its related set of weighted performance items for each one of candidate system type. Among performance items such as commercial speed, minimum radius, costs and so on, we considered also the visual field barrage through a standard approach based on a mixed index of the "visual impact". Such evaluation approach is based on orthogonal projection concepts. The second stage deals with mass transit network evaluation related to the previous chosen innovative system. This is made using of a multicriteria tecnique for appraise alternative network framework hypothesis. For each link candidate to form the system network layout environmental criteria scores are defined with a technique derived from usual ETA studies, while aesthetic and landscape scores are determined as combination of seven factors: visual field barrage value, percentages of observer types in traversed zones, track layout, skiline interference by ground height of the upper surface of the rail of the track, side building distances, surroundings worth, crossed block classification in town masterplan. Each factor score is selected into a semantic scale with seven integer values and a link aesthetic impact score is determined. Considering such scores as random variables, one can also evaluate alternative links and alternative paths of the system under a more objective approach. As an example, conclusions are reported concerning the application of the proposed evaluation planning methodology to a strategic project of a suspended Monorail (Von-Roll MR Ill) in a large area of the city of Florence, Italy. Results also get a great insight into the possibility of routing a consistent part of the assumed Monorail track layout in the open space just over the river-beds of Terzollc torrent and Mugnonc torrent.

Title Strategy behind Hunter Valley track strengthening

Source Permanent Way Institution (PWI)
Conference Permanent Way Institution (PWI) NSW 1985 Annual Convention: The Permanent Way - Says Who?
Publication Date 1985-09-13
Abstract The Way and Works Branch Strategy in the Hunter Valley is to provide the best possible track structure for the type of traffic being carried both present and future. The Up lines in the Hunter Valley presently carry in excess of 25 million tonnes of traffic per annum, mainly composed of coal and wheat. Axle loads on coal trains of 25t are the heaviest in the system and obviously have a disastrous effect on our normal timber sleepered track structure. Abstract This paper describes the influence of axle load (contact pressure) on the adhesion characteristics of wheel and rail in accordance with running speed of vehicle, surface roughness of wheel/rail and wet conditions created by rain, snow, etc.. The adhesion characteristics were evaluated by two measures; one is theoretical analysis with a numerical model adopting the Elasto-Hydrodynamic Lubrication (EHL) theory and the Greenwood-Williamson’s (G-W) stochastic model in which the surface asperity heights of contact surfaces vary according to the Gaussian distribution, and the other is laboratory experiments by means of a twin-disc rolling contact test machine which simulates the contact situation of wheel and rail. The theoretical and experimental results indicate that the adhesion coefficient increases with an increase of the contact pressure in case of smaller surface roughness. In contrast, the adhesion coefficient decreases with an increase of the contact pressure in case of larger surface roughness. Furthermore, the effect of contact pressure on the adhesion characteristics in accordance with surface roughness is affected by the running speed. In the past studies concerning the adhesion characteristic of wheel and rail in railway system by other researcher, a similar tendency of the adhesion coefficient in accordance with contact pressure has been reported. Consequently, it is confident that the influence of axle load on the adhesion coefficient is not proportional, but depends on the surface roughness and the running speed

Title Hygienic environment of railway stations: investigation and countermeasures for reduction of microbial volatile organic compounds (mvocs) generated by fungi in railway stations
 
Subtitle Towards a global railway: proceedings
Author Kyotani, T
Kawasaki, T
Ushiogi, T
Izumi, Y
Fujinami, K
Endo, H
Suzuki, H
Hayakawa, T
Affiliation Railway Technical Research Institute, Kokubunji-shi, Tokyo, Japan
Conference 8th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 18-22 2008
Publication Date 2008
Abstract It was made clear that many railway customers thought the major important factors which influence the comfort of stations were odors and clearness of air. We also found that there was a high correlation between the evaluation of customers about odors and the amount of fungi floating in the air at several points of stations. So we consider that microbial volatile organic compounds (MVOCs) generated by fungi partially cause odors at stations. During the cultivation of Aspergillus versicolor, Penicilluim sp., and Wallemia sp. MVOCs were detected by using SPME (solid phase microextraction) combined with GC-MS (gas chromatography-mass spectrometry). We tried to analyze odorous compounds in the air of an underground platform of a railway station by using the same method. Many compounds could be detected, and some of them were detected during cultivation of fungi. The countermeasure now being thought about for the reduction of odorous compounds and for inhibition of the growth of fungi, is to introduce mirror duct or photocatalyst (TiO2, etc) to railway stations. It is expected that a more comfortable station space can be presented to customers with less labor by introducing them.

Title Indoor air quality at stations: development of a methodology for quantifying railway customers' perception of odor at stations

Affiliation Biotechnology Laboratory and Architecture Laboratory, Railway Technical Research Institute, Tokyo, Japan
Conference 9th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 22-26 2011, Lille, France
Publication Date 2011
Abstract Providing a more comfortable station environment is foremost from the customer's point of view and is essential for sustaining railway customers. As the result of surveys regarding comfort and cleanliness of railway stations and airports, approximately 90% of customers answered that airports are more comfortable and cleaner than railway stations. Customers also have shown a keen interest in indoor air quality and odor in stations. Therefore, the authors commenced to perform surveys to evaluate the indoor air quality of railway stations that integrates customers' views with investigations of airborne microorganisms.

Title Reducing network costs through improved vehicle maintenance: a lean production approach
 
Subtitle Meeting the challenges for future mobility: proceedings
Author Schlake, B
Barkan, CPL
Riley Edwards, J
Affiliation University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering Rail Transportation Technology Center (RailTEC) Urbana, IL, USA
Norfolk Southern Corp. Roanoke, VA
Conference 9th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR) May 22-26 2011, Lille, France
Publication Date 2011
Abstract Railroad yard throughput and terminal performance directly impact overall network capacity. An important element affecting terminal performance is the efficiency at which railcars are inspected and repaired. Technologies are being developed to automate railcar inspections that have the potential to improve railroad terminal efficiency as a result of reduced inspection times and improved rail terminal operations. This research applies Lean Manufacturing methods to the railcar maintenance process and investigates potential means of eliminating waste and reducing variability through the implementation of automated condition monitoring technologies. We consider the potential impact on intermediate 1,000-mile unit-train inspections and quantify the direct and indirect delay costs resulting from railcar inspection. This analysis provides a basis for developing cost-effective inspection and maintenance strategies using automated technologies and evaluates potential benefits in terms of improved efficiency and increased capacity.

Title Research on the layout technology of high-speed railway wind monitoring stations based on data fusion analysis
 
Author Wang, R
Bao, Y
Shen, H
Meng, L
Wang, J
Xu, C
Affiliation China Academy of Railway Sciences Corporation Limited, Beijing, China
Beijing Jiaotong University, Beijing, China
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), railway research to enhance the customer experience, 28 October-1 November 2019, Tokyo, Japan
Publication Date 2019
Abstract The rationality of the setting of railway wind monitoring stations is very important for capturing the key information of the wind, which is the premise of monitoring accuracy for wind motoring/early warning system. A railway wind monitoring stations optimization layout method was proposed, which is based on the analysis of historical wind observation data of the meteorological departments and live data fusion of multidimensional observation equipments. Maximum wind speed along railway was analyzed by historical data. Meanwhile, multi time-space scale analysis model was built to analyze the wind effect to the line, including wind speed, wind direction and wind force, with multi-dimensional observation information of automatic stations, radars and other wind observation equipments. By the historical data analysis and multi time-space scale analysis, the effort of the wind to the line can be obtained, and the setting of wind monitoring stations can be determined. An example is given to illustrate the effectiveness of the method. The method proposed is more precise and reasonable than the traditional method which is relayed on rough meteorological information, site survey and designers’ experience. Moreover, it can also be used for dynamically setting limit restriction sections in strong wind and wind early warning for train operation.

Title Coherent radar system for platform monitoring at railway station
 
Author Fedfeli, E
Cala, V
Ermini, M
Gonnelli, M
Rossetti, A
Affiliation Research and Development Department of RFI, Roma, Italy
Research and Development Department of RFI, Firenze, Italy
Technology Department of RFI, Roma, Italy
Source Prof. C. Cole, Centre for Railway Engineering, Central Queensland University, Rockhampton, Queensland, Australia
Conference 12th World Congress on Railway Research (WCRR), railway research to enhance the customer experience, 28 October-1 November 2019, Tokyo, Japan
Publication Date 2019
Abstract In recent years, railway traffic has grown exponentially and consequently the number of people waiting for trains in station areas has grown. Continuous improvement of personal passenger safety is very important. Therefore, a new integrated system is being developed in order to monitor the crossing of the yellow lines on platforms. This system is based on coherent radar technologies and provides real time alerts in order to detect and alert, through visual and audio signals, the unsafe presence of passengers crossing the limit lines, otherwise called “Yellow Line” and being too close to the railway carriages. The presented system realize real time supervision of passenger along the entire railway platform area, with the aim of reducing the risk of accidents while trains are approaching. The test site has been identified in Bologna Central Station.

Title Assessment of level of service for access and dispersal facilities in a railway terminal
 
Author Bhatnagar, RV
Ram, S
Affiliation Department of Transport Planning, School of Planning and Architecture, New Delhi, India
Journal International Journal of Transportation Science and Technology
Publication Date 2021-04
Abstract Indian Railways has one of the most extensive rail systems in the world which covers 68,000 km of rail track, 7318 stations with over 23 million passengers daily. This public transport system is one of the cheapest modes of travel for millions of Indians daily. With such an extensive system in place, railways need to maintain an efficient infrastructure system; it is expected passengers handling to reach 15.20 Billion by FY20, passenger traffic is noted to grow by 1.69 percent year on year. With such a kind of growth in patronage, it profoundly impacts the existing infrastructure. Thus, a constant upgrade is required in infrastructure. This research study focuses on NSG-1 (Non – Suburban Stations) taking New Delhi station as a case study. The research study is based on the level of service for access and dispersal facilities of the passengers. This paper will analyze the optimum level of service for passengers based on their behavior in a terminal building. This study identifies Equivalent Passenger Units (EPU’s) from passenger behavior and anthropometric studies. EPU’s further is used to evaluate the level of services for the passenger's mobility in the terminal building. Access and Dispersal facilities have been studied for the case study station, and influence zones are created for the same. The outcome of the study is the space assessment. The methodology can be used to identify the optimum level of services for different categories of the railway station. The space planning norms can be generated and then can be used by consultants for efficient planning of brownfield and greenfield stations at the optimum design service level.
